## 1. The continuous view (L42): area law and dimensional transmutation

### 1.1 What it states

**Wilson area law**: for a closed loop `C` in spacetime bounding a minimal surface of area `A(C)`, the Wilson loop expectation decays as

  `⟨W(C)⟩ ≤ K · exp(-σ · A(C))`,

where `σ > 0` is the **string tension** (units of `(mass)²`).

**Dimensional transmutation chain**: pure Yang-Mills generates a single dimensional scale `Λ_QCD` from a dimensionless coupling, via

  `Λ_QCD = μ · exp(-1/(2 · β₀ · g²(μ)))`,

and every dimensional observable is proportional to a power of `Λ_QCD`:

  `m_gap = c_Y · Λ_QCD` (mass-dimension 1),
  `σ = c_σ · Λ_QCD²` (mass-dimension 2),
  ...

### 1.2 What L42 formalises

Five Lean files (`YangMills/L42_LatticeQCDAnchors/`):

- `BetaCoefficients.lean` — `β₀ = (11/3)·N_c`, `β₁ = (34/3)·N_c²`, with universal scheme-independent ratio `β₁/β₀² = 102/121`.
- `RGRunningCoupling.lean` — one-loop running `g²(μ) = 1/(β₀·log(μ/Λ))`; positivity, asymptotic-freedom monotonicity, dimensional transmutation `Λ_QCD = μ·exp(-1/(2β₀g²))`.
- `MassGapFromTransmutation.lean` — `m_gap = c_Y · Λ_QCD`; bridge to project's `ClayYangMillsMassGap`.
- `WilsonAreaLaw.lean` — `WilsonLoopFamily` structure, `HasAreaLaw` predicate, string tension `σ = c_σ · Λ_QCD²`, linear potential `V(r) = σ·r`, structural confinement (`V(r) → ∞`).
- `MasterCapstone.lean` — `PureYangMillsPhysicsChain` bundle + `L42_master_capstone` theorem.

### 1.3 What L42 does not derive

- The numerical values of `c_Y` and `c_σ`. These are accepted as anchor-structure inputs, **not derived from first principles**.
- The actual area-law decay for the SU(N) Yang-Mills Wilson Gibbs measure. This is the **Holy Grail of Confinement** (open since Wilson 1974).
- Higher-loop running (β₂, β₃, ...). The one-loop formula is structural.

## 2. The discrete view (L43): center symmetry and Polyakov loop

### 2.1 What it states

**Center symmetry**: the centre of SU(N) is `Z(SU(N)) ≅ Z_N`, generated by `ω · I` where `ω = exp(2πi/N)` is the primitive N-th root of unity.

**Polyakov loop expectation**: `⟨P⟩` (the trace of the holonomy around a non-contractible time circle) transforms under center as `⟨P⟩ ↦ ω · ⟨P⟩`.

**Order parameter**: 
- **Confined phase**: center symmetry unbroken ⟺ `⟨P⟩ = 0`.
- **Deconfined phase**: center symmetry spontaneously broken ⟺ `⟨P⟩ ≠ 0`.

### 2.2 What L43 formalises

Three Lean files (`YangMills/L43_CenterSymmetry/`):

- `CenterGroup.lean` — `Z_N` primitive root and center elements `ω^k`; cyclicity `(ω^k)^N = 1`; abstract `PolyakovExpectation` structure; `IsConfined` predicate as `⟨P⟩ = 0`.
- `DeconfinementCriterion.lean` — `isConfined_iff_centerInvariant`: equivalence between center invariance and `⟨P⟩ = 0`; SU(2) instances at `ω = -1`; phase dichotomy.
- `MasterCapstone.lean` — `CenterSymmetryConfinementBundle` + `L43_master_capstone` theorem.

### 2.3 What L43 does not derive

- The actual non-zero Polyakov loop expectation `⟨P⟩ ≠ 0` for the deconfined phase (requires Wilson Gibbs measure structure).
- That `centerElement N 1 ≠ 1` for `N ≥ 2` from `Complex.exp` periodicity; this was deferred via hypothesis-conditioning (`ω ≠ 1` as input).
- The actual deconfinement transition at finite temperature `T = T_c` (open work).

## 3. The asymptotic view (L44): 't Hooft 1/N expansion

### 3.1 What it states

**'t Hooft coupling**: `λ = g² · N_c`, held fixed in the limit `N_c → ∞`.

**Reduced beta function**: at one loop,

  `β_λ(λ) = -(11/3) · λ²`,

**N_c-independent** — the N_c-dependence is absorbed into λ. This is the structural reason the 1/N expansion is so powerful at leading order.

**Planar dominance**: in the limit `N_c → ∞`, only **planar (genus-0) Feynman diagrams** contribute at leading order. Non-planar (genus-`g ≥ 1`) diagrams are suppressed by `(1/N²)^g`.

### 3.2 What L44 formalises

Three Lean files (`YangMills/L44_LargeNLimit/`):

- `HooftCoupling.lean` — 't Hooft coupling `λ = g²·N_c`; reduced one-loop `β_λ(λ) = -(11/3)·λ²` (N_c-independent); SU(2)/SU(3) explicit values; bridge to L42's `betaZero`.
- `PlanarDominance.lean` — genus-suppression factor `(1/N²)^g`; planar (g=0) unsuppressed; strict-anti monotonicity; **uniform bound `≤ 1/4`** for `N_c ≥ 2, g ≥ 1`.
- `MasterCapstone.lean` — `LargeNLimitBundle` + `L44_master_capstone` theorem.

### 3.3 What L44 does not derive

- The asymptotic vanishing `genusSuppressionFactor g N_c → 0` as `N_c → ∞`. Requires `Filter.Tendsto` machinery; we substitute the uniform bound `≤ 1/4` (sharp at `N_c = 2, g = 1`).
- Concrete planar Feynman resummation. Requires diagrammatic infrastructure not yet in Mathlib.
- Subleading 1/N corrections.

## 4. The triple-view conceptual bridge

### 4.1 Equivalence claim

In **pure Yang-Mills**, the three views are equivalent statements of confinement:

  **L42 area law (`σ > 0`, `m_gap > 0`)**
  `⟺` (substantively, via the Wilson Gibbs measure)
  **L43 center invariance (`⟨P⟩ = 0`)**
  `⟺` (substantively)
  **L44 confining phase at large N_c (planar resummation finite).**

**Substantive proof of the equivalences**: requires the actual SU(N) Yang-Mills measure analysis. This is **not** what THE-ERIKSSON-PROGRAMME formalises — the project formalises the conditional logical structure of each view independently.

### 4.2 What the Lean blocks formalise

Each block formalises:
- An abstract structure encoding the view's inputs (e.g., `WilsonLoopFamily` for L42, `PolyakovExpectation` for L43, `LargeNLimitBundle` for L44).
- The view's predicate (`HasAreaLaw`, `IsConfined`, `genusSuppressionFactor ≤ 1/4`).
- A master theorem packaging the predicate together with consequences (positivity, monotonicity, etc.).

### 4.3 What the Lean blocks do not formalise

- The substantive equivalences between the three views (requires Wilson Gibbs measure).
- The first-principles values of the dimensionless constants `c_Y`, `c_σ`, planar resummation finite outputs, etc.

## 5. Why three views?

### 5.1 Different mathematical structures emphasised

- **L42** uses the **continuous limit machinery** (renormalisation group flow, dimensional transmutation, Wilson loops as area-law operators). It is the framework most directly connected to Bałaban RG and constructive QFT.
- **L43** uses the **discrete symmetry** (cyclic group `Z_N`, group action on operators). It is the framework most directly connected to lattice gauge theory and the deconfinement phase transition.
- **L44** uses the **asymptotic expansion** (1/N as a small parameter, planar diagrammatics, scheme-independent ratios). It is the framework most directly connected to AdS/CFT and matrix-model methods.

Each view is independently formalisable, and each illuminates a different aspect of the same underlying physics.

### 5.2 Different open problems uncovered by each

- **L42** uncovers: derive `c_Y` and `c_σ` from first principles. **Status**: open since Wilson 1974.
- **L43** uncovers: derive the actual `⟨P⟩ ≠ 0` for deconfined phase. **Status**: numerically observed in lattice QCD; analytically open.
- **L44** uncovers: planar resummation in 4D. **Status**: open; closely related to AdS/CFT correspondence.

The three views uncover three independent open problems whose solutions would each constitute a major advance toward (but not necessarily proving) the Clay Millennium statement.

### 5.3 Connection to the project's literal Clay incondicional %

None of the three blocks **advance** the literal Clay incondicional metric (~32% post-Phase 402). All three are **structural physics anchoring**, parallel to each other, with substantive content deferred via hypothesis-conditioning.

The Clay incondicional metric advances only when:
- A first-principles derivation of `c_Y` or `c_σ` lands.
- A first-principles area-law proof for SU(N) Wilson Gibbs measure lands.
- A first-principles planar resummation in 4D lands.

Each is multi-month or multi-year work; none is "next session" tractable.
