### B. Lattice gauge-observable bridge (`ClayCore/GaugeMarginal.lean`, `GaugeEdgeExpectation.lean`, `WilsonLoopCenter.lean`)

Moves "gauge observables" from the abstract group to the **lattice measure**
`gaugeMeasureFrom μ`:
- `gaugeMeasureFrom_map_eval` — single-edge marginal `= μ`.
- `integral_single_edge` — `∫ f(A e) dμ_gauge = ∫ f dμ`.
- `integral_single_edge_eq_zero` — zero group mean ⟹ zero gauge expectation.
- `gauge_single_edge_trace_eq_zero` — SU(N) instance `∫ tr(A e) dμ_gauge = 0` (N≥2),
  the first lattice Wilson-observable expectation end-to-end.
- `integral_prod_edges` — edge-Fubini factorization `∫ ∏ₑ fₑ(A e) = ∏ₑ ∫ fₑ`.
- `integral_prod_edges_eq_zero` — **scalar** edge-product vanishing (open lines).
- `plaquetteHolonomy_center_smul` — centre eigenvalue `z⁴` (LG6 algebraic core).

**Honesty correction made this session:** the Wilson *loop* is `tr(matrix product)`, NOT
a scalar edge product, so the Fubini vanishing does *not* apply and a closed loop does
*not* vanish (it carries `z⁴` under the centre — the area-law contributor). Scope of
`integral_prod_edges_eq_zero` was tightened accordingly; `HORIZON.md` LG6 records the
caveat. (Avoiding this false "theorem" was itself a key outcome.)

**Open (LG6 remainder → LG8):** `IsMulLeftInvariant (gaugeMeasureFrom μ)` under the
diagonal centre action (instance/transport plumbing, interactive Lean), then the
strong-coupling character expansion (needs Peter–Weyl) and the area law (needs the KP
layer). Lattice only.
