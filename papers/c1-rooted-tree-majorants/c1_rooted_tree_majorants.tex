\begin{abstract}
We present a self-contained, fully machine-verified quantitative
toolkit for cluster expansions of polymer systems with excluded
regions (\emph{holes}), in the discrete geometry of Dimock's
renormalization-group framework.  Three Lean~4 theorems, compiled
against a pinned Mathlib revision with a clean axiom oracle, provide:
(i) a normalized child-factorial bound over spanning trees of the
complete graph (the rooted-tree majorant $\le 4^n$);
(ii) a marked-root leaf summation for the second Ursell expansion with
holes, with the moment constant paid once at the root and closed leaf
ratio $4M^2$ per additional vertex; and
(iii) a target-preserving orderwise bound in which the exact target
union survives until the modified-metric exponential is extracted.
A certified companion (interval arithmetic, 120-bit precision,
committed transcript) tabulates the admissible smallness gate and
encloses every derived constant, including the certified activity
radius $\varepsilon^*$.  Non-vacuity is machine-checked: a concrete
hole family satisfying every hypothesis is exhibited in Lean and both
polymer-facing endpoints are instantiated at it with a strictly
positive weight.  No claim beyond the proved statements is made: this
is expansion tooling with machine constants, originating from (but
logically independent of) a lattice Yang--Mills formalization
programme.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction and honest scope}

Cluster (polymer) expansions convert log-partition functions into sums
over connected geometric objects; their convergence proofs hinge on
combinatorial majorants for tree sums and on geometric smallness
gates.  When the expansion must respect \emph{holes} --- excluded
regions that the expansion sees as atomic units, as in
renormalization-group treatments of large-field regions --- the
bookkeeping is notoriously error-prone: root normalizations, child
factorials, and hole-separation hypotheses interact in ways that have
historically produced wrong constants in the literature\footnote{Our
own development measured exactly such an error in its pre-registered
prediction of the smallness-gate crossover; see
Remark~\ref{rem:crossover}.  The correction protocol --- constants are
computed, never assembled by hand --- is part of the method.}.

This paper's contribution is deliberately narrow and fully witnessed:
\begin{itemize}
\item \textbf{Exact} (Lean theorems, pinned toolchain
  \lean{leanprover/lean4:v4.29.0-rc6}, Mathlib pinned by commit):
  Theorems~\ref{thm:tree}, \ref{thm:markedroot}, \ref{thm:target}, and
  the non-vacuity instantiations of Section~\ref{sec:nonvacuity}.
\item \textbf{Certified} (committed interval-arithmetic transcript,
  \lean{python-flint}/Arb, 120-bit precision, ball-plus-boolean on
  every enclosure): the admissibility table of
  Section~\ref{sec:certified}.
\item No Clay-adjacent or continuum claim of any kind appears.
  Importance is not inherited by thematic proximity; only a proved
  reduction transfers it, and none is claimed here.
\end{itemize}

\section{The discrete geometry}

\begin{definition}[cube lattice and adjacency]
For $d, L \in \mathbb{N}$, a \emph{cube} is a point of
$(\mathbb{Z}/L)^d$ (Lean: \lean{Cube d L := Fin d $\to$ ZMod L}).  Two
cubes are adjacent (\lean{cubeAdj}) iff they are distinct and each
coordinate differs by $0$ or $\pm 1$: the sup-metric neighbor graph,
each cube having $3^d - 1$ neighbors.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[hole family]
A \emph{hole family} (Lean: \lean{HoleFamily d L}) is a finite set of
finite sets of cubes --- the connected components of the excluded
region.  A polymer $X$ \emph{respects} the family iff every hole is
contained in $X$ or disjoint from it.  The bounds below hypothesize:
pairwise disjointness of holes, no \lean{cubeAdj} edge between
distinct holes, and non-emptiness of each hole.
\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[weights and constants]\label{def:constants}
For a rate $\kappa$, the hole weight of a polymer $X$ is
$e^{-\kappa\,(\rho(X)+1)}$ with $\rho$ the discrete modified metric
(Lean: \lean{appendixFHoleExpWeight}).  The smallness gate at
dimension $d$ and rate $\kappa_0$ is
\begin{equation}\label{eq:gate}
G(d,\kappa_0) \;=\; (3^d)^2\, e^{-\kappa_0}\, 2^{3^d+1} \;<\; 1
\qquad (\text{Lean hypothesis } h_{Cq}),
\end{equation}
and the derived constants are
\begin{equation}\label{eq:constants}
\mathrm{RS} = (1-G)^{-1}, \qquad
M = \max\!\bigl(1,\ \kappa_0^{-1},\ \mathrm{RS}\bigr), \qquad
\Lambda = 4M^2,
\end{equation}
(Lean: \lean{appendixFSecondUrsellMomentConstant},
\lean{appendixFSecondUrsellLeafConstant}).  The geometric series
downstream has ratio $\Lambda\varepsilon$ in the activity size
$\varepsilon$, so $\varepsilon^* = \Lambda^{-1}$ is the certified
activity radius.
\end{definition}

\section{The three endpoints (exact)}

All three are stable aliases in module \lean{MarkedRootedClosure}
(file \lean{MarkedRootedClosure/PaperTheorems.lean}); each is an exact
application of a pinned upstream theorem --- no new axiom, no opaque
assumption.

\begin{theorem}[rooted-tree majorant;
\lean{normalizedRootedChildFactorialTreeBound}]\label{thm:tree}
For every $n \in \mathbb{N}$,
\[
\frac{n+1}{(n+1)!}
\sum_{T \in \mathcal{T}(K_{n+1})} \ \prod_{v} \bigl(c_T(v)\bigr)!
\;\le\; 4^n,
\]
where $\mathcal{T}(K_{n+1})$ is the set of spanning trees of the
complete graph on $n+1$ vertices and $c_T(v)$ the child count of $v$
in $T$ rooted at the marked vertex.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[exact value --- upgrade slot]\label{rem:catalan}
The left-hand sum satisfies the \emph{exact identity}
$\sum_{T} \prod_v c_T(v)! = n!\,C_n$ with $C_n$ the $n$th Catalan
number, machine-proved in the companion repository
\lean{rooted-tree-catalan-closure}; since
$(n+1)\,n!\,C_n/(n+1)! = C_n \le 4^n$, Theorem~\ref{thm:tree} is its
corollary, and the majorant overcounts by only the factor
$4^n/C_n \sim \sqrt{\pi}\, n^{3/2}$.

module compiles green in the pinned core of this repository (merge
tracked under judge J-C1-1); until then it is cited, not claimed}
\end{remark}

\begin{theorem}[marked-root leaf summation;
\lean{markedRootLeafGeometricBound}]\label{thm:markedroot}
Let $HF$ be a hole family in dimension $d$ with the separation
hypotheses above, $w \ge 0$ a polymer weight dominated by the hole
weight at rate $2\kappa_0$, $r$ a marked root cube, and suppose the
gate \eqref{eq:gate}.  Then for every $n$,
\[
(n+1) \cdot S^{\#}_{HF,w,r}(n) \;\le\; M \cdot \Lambda^{n},
\]
where $S^{\#}$ is the weighted marked-root tree sum of the second
Ursell expansion (Lean:
\lean{appendixFHoleHsharpWeightedTreeMarkedRootSum}) and $M, \Lambda$
are the constants of \eqref{eq:constants}: the moment constant is paid
\emph{once} at the root; each additional vertex costs exactly the
closed leaf ratio $\Lambda = 4M^2$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[target-preserving orderwise bound;
\lean{targetPreservingWeightedTreeBound}]\label{thm:target}
With the same geometric hypotheses, let $w \le
e^{-\mathrm{rate}\,(\rho+1)} \cdot u$ be a split of the weight into an
extracted exponential at a nonnegative rate times a remainder $u$
dominated by the hole weight at rate $2\kappa_0$, and let $Y$ be a
target union whose skeleton contains a root.  Then
\[
T^{\#}_{HF,w,Y}(n) \;\le\; M \cdot e^{-\mathrm{rate}\,(\rho(Y)+1)}
\cdot \Lambda^{n}.
\]
The exact target union remains present until the modified-metric
exponential has been extracted; only then is the marked-root estimate
applied --- the orderwise bookkeeping that hole-respecting expansions
require.
\end{theorem}

\begin{remark}[a measured registration failure]\label{rem:crossover}
The pre-registered charter predicted crossovers $15.72$ and $32.60$
from a hand-assembled expression carrying $(3^d)^4$ where the gate
\eqref{eq:gate} carries $(3^d)^2$.  The certified run measured the
discrepancy (transcript, ``crossover audit'': \textsc{registered
prediction does not re-derive}); the registered admissibility bands
$\kappa_0 \ge 16$ ($d{=}2$) and $\kappa_0 \ge 33$ ($d{=}3$) remain
true --- sufficient, not sharp --- and only certified values are inked
here.  We record the failure because the method requires it:
constants are computed, never assembled.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-vacuity, machine-checked}\label{sec:nonvacuity}

The vacuity failure mode of formalized expansion bounds is hypotheses
that no concrete geometry satisfies.  Module
\lean{MarkedRootedClosure/NonVacuity.lean} exhibits --- as instance
terms, not existentials --- the hole family $HF^{w}$ in $d = 2$,
$L = 8$ with two singleton holes at sup-distance $4$, and proves by
kernel decision (\lean{decide}) that the holes are disjoint,
edge-separated, and nonempty; the gate \eqref{eq:gate} at
$\kappa_0 = 16$ is proved in Lean via $e^{16} > 2.7^{16} > 82944$
--- the Lean-exact counterpart of the certified row
$G \approx 0.00933$.  Both polymer endpoints are then instantiated at
$HF^{w}$ with the \emph{strictly positive} weight
$e^{-32(\rho+1)}$ (no zero-weight degeneracy):
\lean{nonvacuous\_markedRootLeafGeometricBound} and
\lean{nonvacuous\_targetPreservingWeightedTreeBound}.
