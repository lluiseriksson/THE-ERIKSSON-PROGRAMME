\begin{abstract}
The Amos-type upper bound on the modified-Bessel ratio,
$I_{\nu+1}(x)/I_\nu(x) < x/(\nu + \tfrac12 + \sqrt{(\nu+\tfrac12)^2 +
x^2})$, has a distinguished algebraic property: its right-hand side
$U$ satisfies the exact calibration identity $1/U - U =
(2\nu+1)/x$.  From that identity alone --- by ordered-field algebra,
with no further analytic input --- follow a unit-step inequality
$\rho_\nu - \rho_{\nu+1} < 1/x$ for consecutive ratios, the
strict increase of the log-derivative $(\log I_\nu)' = \rho_\nu +
\nu/x$ across orders, and the strict monotonicity of a
$\varphi$-sequence arising in a two-dimensional lattice-gauge
surface expansion.  We formalize this calculus in Lean~4: a single
module defines the bound once (\lean{AmosBound}) and proves the
calibration engine and four consequence theorems through that one
definition, together with two rational satisfiability witnesses
whose Amos hypothesis holds by exact Pythagorean arithmetic; all
fifteen Lean statements of the development pass the axiom oracle
with exactly
$[\lean{propext}, \lean{Classical.choice}, \lean{Quot.sound}]$
against a pinned Mathlib.  A certified companion (256-bit interval
arithmetic, self-contained series-plus-tail enclosures, committed
transcript) certifies the bound provably strictly at all $1206$
points of a pre-registered grid covering the arguments the
applications consume.  A Bessel interface completes the closure:
integer-order $I_n$ is defined by its power series in the same
pinned development, with positivity and the three-term recurrence
proved as theorems, so the consequence theorems hold for genuine
Bessel ratios with the Amos bound as the single remaining
hypothesis.  The scope is stated exactly: the Amos bound
itself remains a classical cited theorem taken as hypothesis ---
this paper unifies its three previously scattered uses in our
formal development into one named proposition with one oracle and
one certified numerical witness, and no downstream result changes
its verification class.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

Cluster and character expansions of two-dimensional lattice gauge
theory run on ratios of modified Bessel functions: for gauge group
$SU(2)$ the mean plaquette is $I_2(\beta)/I_1(\beta)$ in closed form,
the string tension is $-\log(I_2/I_1)$, and monotonicity structures
of expansions in the heat-kernel/von Mises family reduce to
inequalities between consecutive ratios
$\rho_\nu = I_{\nu+1}/I_\nu$.  The workhorse
inequality is the Amos-type upper bound \cite{Amos74,Segura11,RS16}
\begin{equation}\label{eq:amos}
\rho_\nu(x) \;<\; \RHS(\nu, x) \;=\;
\frac{x}{\nu + \tfrac12 + \sqrt{(\nu+\tfrac12)^2 + x^2}}
\qquad (x > 0,\ \nu \ge 0),
\end{equation}
with $\RHS$ as in Definition~\ref{def:amosbound} below.

We call a statement that assumes \eqref{eq:amos} as an explicit
hypothesis a \emph{consumer} of the bound, and a \emph{site} a place
in the programme where such a statement --- or its numerical
counterpart --- lives.  The formal developments happen in a central
repository whose Lean toolchain and Mathlib commit are fixed
(\emph{pinned}); a file checked against any other toolchain state is
\emph{off-pin}.  In this programme, \eqref{eq:amos} entered three
sites as a \emph{named hypothesis} --- an explicit assumption of a
machine-checked theorem, never silently used:
(a) the surface track's $\varphi$-monotonicity lemma, whose Lean core
carried \lean{hAmos} verbatim and on which the surface theorem's
$E' < 0$ for $0 < \beta \le 3.5$ is conditional;
(b) the Feynman--Hellmann unit-step development for $(\log I_\nu)'$
\cite{ErikssonFH26}, whose Lean core carried \lean{hamos} verbatim;
(c) the exactly soluble string-tension computation, whose
independent numerical program (external repository
\lean{ym-lattice-numerics}) computes precisely the ratios of
\eqref{eq:amos} --- with no Amos check at all.
Copies (a) and (b) were standalone files machine-checked with Lean
4.30.0-rc2 and a Mathlib snapshot \emph{different} from the central
repository's pin --- statements shared by textual identity, not by a
common definition, with no single artifact certifying both.

This paper contributes:
\begin{enumerate}
\item a single Lean module \lean{AmosClosure} in the pinned central
  core defining \eqref{eq:amos} once
  (Definition~\ref{def:amosbound}) and proving the calibration
  engine (Lemmas~\ref{lem:calibration}, \ref{lem:small}) and all
  four consumer theorems (Theorems~\ref{thm:phiunit}--\ref{thm:logd})
  --- Lemma~\ref{lem:calibration} generic by design, the other five
  through the single definition --- the toolchain-fragmentation gap
  closed, with a clean axiom oracle on all fifteen statements
  including the Bessel interface of Section~\ref{sec:interface};
\item rational non-vacuity witnesses (Section~\ref{sec:nonvacuity})
  in which the Amos hypothesis holds by \emph{exact} arithmetic
  (a Pythagorean calibration point makes the square root rational);
\item a certified companion (Section~\ref{sec:certified}) certifying
  \eqref{eq:amos} provably strictly at all $1206$ points of a
  pre-registered grid covering the domain the consumers actually
  consume.
\end{enumerate}
Statuses are kept separate throughout: \textbf{exact} means a Lean
theorem in the pinned central core with axiom oracle
$[\lean{propext}, \lean{Classical.choice}, \lean{Quot.sound}]$;
\textbf{certified} means a committed interval-arithmetic transcript
with ball-plus-boolean output; \textbf{paper-level} means ordinary
prose, always flagged.  The formal development lives in a repository
whose top-level namespace (\lean{YangMills/}) reflects its original
motivation in lattice gauge theory; no reduction to any continuum or
gauge-theoretic problem is claimed or used, and --- stated plainly
--- \emph{no downstream theorem changes its verification class by
this paper}: consumers of \eqref{eq:amos} were conditional on a
classical bound before and remain so; what improves is that the
condition is now one audited object instead of two copies plus an unchecked numerical site.

\section{Setting}\label{sec:setting}

Throughout, $I_\nu$ is the modified Bessel function of the first
kind; for $x > 0$ and $\nu \ge 0$ it is positive, and consecutive
orders satisfy the three-term recurrence
$I_{\nu-1}(x) - I_{\nu+1}(x) = \tfrac{2\nu}{x} I_\nu(x)$
\cite[10.29.1]{DLMF}.  Write $\rho_\nu = I_{\nu+1}/I_\nu$.  These
facts are classical background (paper-level here); the Lean theorems
below take the recurrence and the positivity of the relevant values
as explicit hypotheses and are, as formal objects, statements of
ordered-field algebra plus one square root.

\begin{definition}[the bound, defined once]\label{def:amosbound}
For $\nu, x, r \in \mathbb{R}$,
\[
\RHS(\nu,x) = \frac{x}{\nu + \tfrac12 + \sqrt{(\nu+\tfrac12)^2 + x^2}}
\quad (\text{Lean: } \lean{amosRHS}), \qquad
\lean{AmosBound}\ \nu\ x\ r \;:\iff\; r < \RHS(\nu, x).
\]
Every consumer theorem below
(Theorems~\ref{thm:phiunit}--\ref{thm:logd}, and
Lemma~\ref{lem:small}) hypothesizes \lean{AmosBound} --- never a
textual copy of the right-hand side; the calibration engine
(Lemma~\ref{lem:calibration}) is deliberately generic in its first
argument and does not mention the definition.  The truth of
$\lean{AmosBound}\ \nu\ x\ \rho_\nu(x)$ for actual
Bessel ratios is the classical theorem of Amos \cite{Amos74} (sharp
forms: \cite{Segura11,RS16}); it is \emph{not} formalized here and
remains the single analytic input of every consumer.
\end{definition}

\section{Main results}\label{sec:results}

All statements are in module \lean{AmosClosure} (file
\lean{AmosClosure/Core.lean}) of the central repository, compiled
against its pinned toolchain; the recorded build and oracle facts are
in Section~\ref{sec:repro}.

\begin{lemma}[calibration engine;
\lean{amos\_calibration}]\label{lem:calibration}
Let $a, b > 0$ and $0 < t < b/(a + \sqrt{a^2 + b^2})$.  Then
\[
\frac{2a}{b} \;<\; \frac{1}{t} - t .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[Amos smallness; \lean{amos\_small}]\label{lem:small}
If $\nu \ge 0$, $x > 0$, $r > 0$ and $\lean{AmosBound}\ \nu\ x\ r$,
then $r < x/(2\nu+1)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{theorem}[$\varphi$ unit step;
\lean{phi\_unit\_step}]\label{thm:phiunit}
Let $m \ge 1$, $b > 0$, $u > 0$ with $\lean{AmosBound}\ m\ b\ u$.
Then
\[
\Bigl(\frac{1}{u^2} - u^2\Bigr)
- \frac{2}{b}\Bigl((m-1)u + \frac{m+2}{u}\Bigr)
+ \frac{4(2m+1)}{b^2} \;>\; 0 .
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[$\varphi$-monotonicity step;
\lean{phi\_step\_of\_recurrences}]\label{thm:phistep}
Let $m \ge 1$, $b > 0$, and let $R_0 > 0$, $R_1 > 0$, $R_2$ satisfy
the two recurrence relations
$1/R_0 = R_1 + 2m/b$ and $R_2 = 1/R_1 - 2(m+1)/b$
(the shapes taken by $\rho_{m-1}, \rho_m, \rho_{m+1}$ under
\cite[10.29.1]{DLMF}) together with $\lean{AmosBound}\ m\ b\ R_1$.
Then
\[
\varphi_m \;<\; \varphi_{m+1},
\qquad\text{where}\qquad
\varphi_m = \frac{(m-1)/R_0^2 + (m+1)R_1^2}{m}.
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[unit step;
\lean{unit\_step\_of\_recurrence\_and\_amos}]\label{thm:unitstep}
Let $x > 0$, $\nu \ge 0$, $I_0, I_1 > 0$ and $I_2$ satisfy
$I_0 - I_2 = \tfrac{2(\nu+1)}{x} I_1$ and
$\lean{AmosBound}\ \nu\ x\ (I_1/I_0)$.  Then
\[
\frac{I_1}{I_0} - \frac{I_2}{I_1} \;<\; \frac{1}{x}
\]
(positivity of $I_2$ is not needed).
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[log-derivative unit step;
\lean{logderiv\_unit\_step\_increase}]\label{thm:logd}
Under the hypotheses of Theorem~\ref{thm:unitstep}, in the closed
form $(\log I_\nu)' = \rho_\nu + \nu/x$,
\[
\Bigl(\frac{I_2}{I_1} + \frac{\nu+1}{x}\Bigr)
- \Bigl(\frac{I_1}{I_0} + \frac{\nu}{x}\Bigr) \;>\; 0 .
\]
\end{theorem}

\section{Proof architecture}\label{sec:architecture}

The formal proofs are the authority; the mechanism is short enough to
give almost in full.

\emph{One identity drives everything.}  Let $s = \sqrt{a^2+b^2}$ and
$U = b/(a+s)$.  Rationalizing, $U = (s-a)/b$ (from
$(s-a)(s+a) = b^2$), and therefore
\[
\frac{1}{U} - U \;=\; \frac{s+a}{b} - \frac{s-a}{b} \;=\; \frac{2a}{b}
\]
\emph{exactly}.  Since $t \mapsto 1/t - t$ is strictly decreasing on
$(0,\infty)$, any $t < U$ satisfies $1/t - t > 2a/b$: that is
Lemma~\ref{lem:calibration}, and with $a = \nu + \tfrac12$, $b = x$
it converts the Amos bound into the calibrated inequality
$1/\rho - \rho > (2\nu+1)/x$.  The unit step
(Theorem~\ref{thm:unitstep}) is then one line: the recurrence gives
$I_0/I_1 - I_2/I_1 = 2(\nu+1)/x$ exactly, and subtracting the
calibrated inequality leaves exactly $1/x$ of room.
Theorem~\ref{thm:logd} is the same statement re-indexed.

\emph{The $\varphi$ step.}  Writing $u = R_1$ for the candidate
$\rho_m$, the difference $\varphi_{m+1} - \varphi_m$ reduces, after
substituting both recurrences and clearing denominators, to the exact
algebraic identity
\[
(m/R_1^2 + (m+2)R_2^2)\,m - ((m-1)/R_0^2 + (m+1)R_1^2)(m+1)
\;=\; 2m(m+1)\,\delta,
\]
\[
\delta
= \Bigl(\frac1{u^2} - u^2\Bigr)
- \frac2b\Bigl((m-1)u + \frac{m+2}{u}\Bigr) + \frac{4(2m+1)}{b^2}
= \Bigl(u + \frac1u - \frac3b\Bigr)
  \Bigl(\Bigl(\frac1u - u\Bigr) - \frac{2m+1}{b}\Bigr)
+ \frac{2m+1}{b^2}.
\]
The second factor of the product is positive by the calibrated
inequality; the first by the smallness $u < b/(2m+1) \le b/3$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:small}, itself one comparison of denominators); the
additive term is positive outright.  That is
Theorems~\ref{thm:phiunit} and \ref{thm:phistep}.  Both exact
identities are discharged in Lean by \lean{field\_simp; ring}; the
inequalities by \lean{nlinarith}/\lean{linarith} over the ordered
field.  No analytic fact about $I_\nu$ enters anywhere: the analytic
content is exactly the hypothesis \lean{AmosBound} plus the
recurrence shapes.

\begin{remark}[a measured enclosure defect, found by audit]
\label{rem:tail}
The first companion run used the remainder majorant
$t_K \cdot q/(1-q)$, which bounds only the terms \emph{after} the
first omitted one: $t_K$ itself was in no bucket, so the $I_n$
enclosures provably failed containment of the true values (by
$\approx t_K$, at most $6.5\times 10^{-34}$ relative on the grid).
The independent numeric audit caught the defect and measured its
direction: at every grid point where it is visible, both enclosures
undershoot with the denominator undershooting relatively more, so
the computed ratio lies \emph{above} the true ratio and every
\textsc{pass} verdict of run 1 was a true statement reached by an
unsound documented method.  The majorant was corrected to
$t_K/(1-q)$, the run repeated (identical printed values: the
correction is below the $10^{-16}$ print precision), and both
transcripts are committed --- run 1 marked superseded, never
deleted.  We record the failure as evidence for the audit protocol:
enclosure methods are audited for containment, not only for
verdicts.
\end{remark}

\section{Non-vacuity, machine-checked}\label{sec:nonvacuity}

Both consumer families are instantiated in Lean at concrete rational
values (file \lean{NonVacuity.lean} of the module) --- instance
terms, not existentials.  The calibration point is chosen Pythagorean:
at $a = \nu + \tfrac12 = \tfrac32$ (order $\nu = 1$) and argument
$x = 2$,
$\sqrt{(\tfrac32)^2 + 2^2} = \sqrt{\tfrac{25}{4}} = \tfrac52$
\emph{exactly}, so $\RHS = \tfrac12$ exactly and the Amos hypothesis
for the witness ratio $\tfrac25$ is the rational comparison
$\tfrac25 < \tfrac12$:
\begin{itemize}
\item $\varphi$ family (\lean{nonvacuous\_phi\_step}): $m=1$, $b=2$,
  $(R_0, R_1, R_2) = (\tfrac57, \tfrac25, \tfrac12)$; both
  recurrences hold by rational arithmetic, and the conclusion
  $\varphi_1 = \tfrac{8}{25} < \tfrac72 = \varphi_2$ is strict.
\item unit-step family (\lean{nonvacuous\_unit\_step}): $\nu=1$,
  $x=2$, $(I_0, I_1, I_2) = (1, \tfrac25, \tfrac15)$; the recurrence
  is $1 - \tfrac15 = \tfrac45 = \tfrac{2\cdot 2}{2}\cdot\tfrac25$,
  and the conclusion $\tfrac25 - \tfrac12 < \tfrac12$ is strict.
\end{itemize}
What this proves: the hypothesis sets of all four consumer theorems
are satisfiable with all-positive values and a strict Amos margin, so
the theorems have non-trivial instances.  What it does not prove:
the witnesses are not true Bessel values (the certified companion is
what ties the hypotheses to actual ratios), and satisfiability is not
sharpness.

\section{The Bessel interface}\label{sec:interface}

The preceding sections treat the recurrence shapes as hypotheses.
This section removes that layer: we define integer-order modified
Bessel functions by their power series \emph{in the pinned core} and
prove the analytic facts the consumers need, so that the recurrence
hypotheses are discharged and \lean{AmosBound} --- now a statement
about genuine Bessel ratios --- remains the sole named hypothesis.
(File \lean{BesselInterface.lean} of the module.)

\begin{definition}[\lean{besselI}]\label{def:besselI}
For $n \in \mathbb{N}$ and $x \in \mathbb{R}$,
\[
\lean{besselI}\ n\ x \;=\; \sum_{k=0}^{\infty}
\frac{(x/2)^{n+2k}}{k!\,(n+k)!},
\]
as a \lean{tsum} over the summable term family (summability for all
$x \ge 0$ by comparison with the exponential series, via
$(n+k)! \ge n!\,k!$'s consequence $(n+k)! \ge n!$; Lean:
\lean{summable\_besselTerm}).
\end{definition}

\begin{theorem}[positivity; \lean{besselI\_pos}]\label{thm:besselpos}
For every $n$ and $x > 0$, $\lean{besselI}\ n\ x > 0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[the recurrence as a theorem;
\lean{besselI\_recurrence}]\label{thm:besselrec}
For every $n$ and $x > 0$,
\[
\lean{besselI}\ n\ x - \lean{besselI}\ (n+2)\ x
\;=\; \frac{2(n+1)}{x}\, \lean{besselI}\ (n+1)\ x
\]
--- DLMF 10.29.1 at order $n+1$, proved from the series by the
termwise identity
$\tfrac{2(n+1)}{x} t_{n+1,k} = t_{n,k} - t_{n+2,k-1}$ (with
$t_{n+2,-1} := 0$) and an index shift of the summed family.
\end{theorem}

\begin{theorem}[true-Bessel consumers]
\label{thm:besselconsumers}
Let $x > 0$ and write
$\rho_n = \lean{besselI}\ (n+1)\ x / \lean{besselI}\ n\ x$.
If $\lean{AmosBound}$ holds at $(n, x, \rho_n)$, then
$\rho_n - \rho_{n+1} < 1/x$ and the log-derivative unit step of
Theorem~\ref{thm:logd} holds; if it holds at
$(m+1, x, \rho_{m+1})$, then $\varphi_{m+1} < \varphi_{m+2}$ in the
true-ratio $\varphi$-sequence.  No recurrence hypothesis appears:
both recurrence shapes are instances of
Theorem~\ref{thm:besselrec}.  (Lean names:
\lean{besselI\_unit\_step}, \lean{besselI\_logderiv\_step},
\lean{besselI\_phi\_step}.)
\end{theorem}

What remains hypothetical after this section is exactly one
statement: $\lean{AmosBound}\ n\ x\ \rho_n(x)$ for the true ratios
--- the classical bound \eqref{eq:amos} itself.  Its formalization
over \lean{besselI} is the natural next arc; everything downstream
of it in this paper is now theorem.

\section{Related work}\label{sec:related}

Bounds of the form \eqref{eq:amos} originate with Amos
\cite{Amos74}, who also gave the computational scheme for the ratios;
Segura \cite{Segura11} derived sharp two-sided bounds and the
associated Tur\'an-type inequalities; Hornik and Gr\"un
\cite{HG13} studied the Amos-type family systematically;
Ruiz-Antol\'in and Segura \cite{RS16} gave the sharp family at both
ends of the parameter range; and Hornik and Gr\"un \cite{HG24}
treat the generalized Amos-type family
$t/(\alpha + \sqrt{\beta t^2 + \gamma^2})$, showing in particular
that Amos's \emph{upper} bound cannot be improved within that
family's parameter freedom.  Why this programme carries
\eqref{eq:amos} rather than a sharper bound: the consumers need the
\emph{exact} calibration $1/U - U = (2\nu+1)/x$ of
Section~\ref{sec:architecture}, an identity available precisely for
the Amos form; sharper bounds \cite{Segura11,RS16} trade that
algebraic exactness for tightness, and by \cite{HG24} the Amos upper
bound is already optimal within its own family --- so for
consequence-mining of this kind it is the natural choice, not a
convenience.  The recurrence is \cite[10.29.1]{DLMF}.  On the programme
side, the prose companions of the two Lean consumer families are the
author's preprints \cite{ErikssonFH26} (whose ``machine-checked
core'' is precisely the file ported here) and \cite{ErikssonVM26}
(the surface-expansion closure map consuming the $\varphi$-lemma);
a weighted Tur\'an-type variant via the calibrated Amos bound is
\cite{ErikssonTuran26}.  The formalization builds on Mathlib
\cite{mathlib20}.  We are not aware of prior \emph{formalized}
treatments of Amos-type ratio bounds or their expansion-theoretic
consumers; readers aware of one are invited to point us to it.

\section{Conclusion}

The machine-checked development routes both Lean consumer families of
the Amos bound through one audited definition in one pinned core,
with rational satisfiability witnesses; a certified 1206-point
numerical witness covers the numerical site on the consumed domain.  Limitations, stated where
they arise: the bound \eqref{eq:amos} itself is classical and not
formalized --- with the interface of Section~\ref{sec:interface} in
place it is the \emph{only} analytic input left, now a statement
about the in-core \lean{besselI}, and proving it there is the
natural next formal target; the grid certification is pointwise, not
uniform; no downstream theorem changes its verification class.
Reasonable next uses: consuming \lean{AmosBound} and the
\lean{besselI} interface directly in any future surface-track or
transfer-matrix formalization, and upstreaming the modified-Bessel
API to Mathlib.
