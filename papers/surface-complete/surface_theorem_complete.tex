\begin{abstract}
For $\beta>0$ let $I_m=I_m(\beta)$ denote modified Bessel functions of the
first kind, and set $a_m = I_m^2\,[(m-1)I_{m-1}^2+(m+1)I_{m+1}^2]$,
$b_m = m\,I_m^4$, $\FA(t)=\sum_{m\ge1}a_m\sin(mt)$,
$\FB(t)=\sum_{m\ge1}b_m\sin(mt)$, and $E=\FA/(2\FB)$. The global
ratio-monotonicity problem for the surface expansion of a two-dimensional
$SU(2)$ lattice gauge observable is: (i) $\FB>0$ on $(0,\pi)$, and (ii)
$E'<0$ on $(0,\pi)$, for every $\beta>0$. We prove (i) twice, and prove (ii) for $0<\beta\le3$ via an exact
pair-identity skeleton plus an archived interval certificate executed by
two independent interval-arithmetic implementations. The
structural core is exact: $E$ is, as an algebraic identity, the mean of
$\cos\psi$ under the midpoint law of a four-step killed von Mises
bridge; the generating kernels reduce, via the Neumann addition theorem,
to two-dimensional integrals of a \emph{single} Bessel function whose
saddle deficit is an exact sum of two squares; and exact saddle
cancellations yield the coefficients of the (verified) closed
second-order law $E=\cos(t/2)(1-c(t)/\beta)+O(\beta^{-2})$,
$c(t)=(4\cos^2(t/4)-1)/(2\cos(t/4)\cos(t/2))$. Three certified negative
results (interval arithmetic, two implementations, nested enclosures)
kill every monotone full-path coupling, with an exact mechanism at
threshold $\beta|\cos t|=3/2$. For the remaining range we give a
two-scale closure map with a verified candidate threshold $\beta_0=15$
and, at the $\pi$-endpoint, exact identities for the cubic coefficient
$c_3$ (telescoped alternating form, integral form, parity) together with
its verified prefactor law. Every claim is labelled exact / certified / verified; the
machine-checked lemmas are Lean~4/Mathlib, machine-checked modulo
classical Bessel inputs carried as named hypotheses.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}

This paper consolidates the resolution status of a two-part positivity
problem that arises as the last analytic gap in a surface (character)
expansion for two-dimensional $SU(2)$ lattice gauge theory
\cite{Companion,PhiLemma,WronskianNote}. The problem is stated entirely in
terms of modified Bessel functions and trigonometric series, and we treat
it as such; no claim of physical consequence beyond the stated expansion
is made, and importance is not inherited by thematic proximity.

Throughout, $\beta>0$, $I_m=I_m(\beta)$, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:coeffs}
a_m = I_m^2\bigl[(m-1)I_{m-1}^2+(m+1)I_{m+1}^2\bigr],\qquad
b_m = m\,I_m^4,\qquad m\ge1 .
\end{equation}
Both sequences decay super-exponentially, so
\begin{equation}\label{eq:FAFB}
\FA(t)=\sum_{m\ge1}a_m\sin(mt),\qquad
\FB(t)=\sum_{m\ge1}b_m\sin(mt),\qquad
E(t)=\frac{\FA(t)}{2\FB(t)},
\end{equation}
are real-analytic on $\mathbb{R}$, and the Wronskian
$W=2(\FA'\FB-\FA\FB')$ satisfies $W=4\FB^2\,(E)'$ wherever $\FB\neq0$
\cite{WronskianNote}.

\begin{conjecture}[global ratio monotonicity]\label{conj:main}
For every $\beta>0$: \textup{(i)} $\FB>0$ on $(0,\pi)$;
\textup{(ii)} $E'<0$ on $(0,\pi)$.
\end{conjecture}

Part (i) is proved below (twice). Part (ii) is proved for $0<\beta\le3$
(Theorem~\ref{thm:B}; exact skeleton plus the archived certificate
\texttt{certify\_thmB.py} and its Arb twin), is supported by an exact
structural reduction and a verified truth map for $\beta\ge15$, and is
otherwise open; Section~\ref{sec:closure} states the precise two-scale
closure map. The conjecture was born inside this programme (it is the
global form of an $\varepsilon$-window positivity required by the surface
expansion); no external pedigree is claimed.

\begin{lemma}[exact; sinc positivity]\label{lem:sinc}
Let $1\le m<n$ and $T_{mn}(t)=(m-n)\sin((m+n)t)+(m+n)\sin((n-m)t)$
as in Section~\textup{4}. Then $T_{mn}(t)>0$ for
$0<t\le\pi/(m+n)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $s=m+n$, $p=n-m\ge1$, so $T_{mn}(t)=s\sin(pt)-p\sin(st)$.
Since $\sin(x)/x$ is strictly decreasing on $(0,\pi]$ and
$0<pt<st\le\pi$, we get $\sin(pt)/(pt)>\sin(st)/(st)$, i.e.\
$st\sin(pt)>pt\sin(st)$; dividing by $t>0$ gives
$s\sin(pt)>p\sin(st)$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[{relay criterion for $\beta\in[6,15]$}]
\label{lem:relaycrit}
Let $\langle f\rangle=\iint_{[-\pi,\pi]^2}Kf\,ds\,d\alpha$ with
$K=I_1(2\beta R)/R>0$, and let $\bar E$ be any rational constant. Then
$E'<0$ at $(t,\beta)$ if and only if $\langle D\rangle>0$ and
\[
W_c:=\langle N_t\rangle\langle D\rangle
+\langle (N-\bar E D)\,\partial_t\!\log K\rangle\langle D\rangle
-\bigl(\langle N\rangle-\bar E\langle D\rangle\bigr)
\langle D\,\partial_t\!\log K\rangle<0
\]
(the $\bar E$-terms cancel identically). The certificate encloses the
five integrals by exp-factored interval cells: $z=2\beta R$ is
linearized at the exact rational cell center with interval-gradient
remainder; the linear exponential integrates exactly; and the two
scaled Bessel factors are
$A(z)=e^{-z}I_1(z)/z$ --- strictly decreasing, since
$(\log A)'=I_0/I_1-1-2/z<0$ follows from the classical lower bound
at $\nu=0$ (Amos \cite{Amos}; \cite{Segura2011}, Thm.~1) and
$\sqrt{1+z^2}\le z+1$ ---
enclosed by pure endpoint evaluation, and $B(z)=e^{-z}H_B(z)$, where
\[
H_B(z):=\frac{I_0(z)-2I_1(z)/z}{z^2}=\frac{I_2(z)}{z^2},\qquad
H_B(0)=\tfrac18
\]
\textup{(}the second equality is the recurrence
\cite[10.29.1]{DLMF} at $\nu=1$; positive-coefficient entire
series; the subscript
distinguishes it from the entry-law kernel $H(\psi)$\textup{)},
enclosed unconditionally by $[e^{-z_{\rm hi}}H_B(z_{\rm lo}),
e^{-z_{\rm lo}}H_B(z_{\rm hi})]$; the identity
$K\,\partial_t\!\log K=32\beta^3c\,c'\,(1-P-Q)H_B(z)$, $c=\cos(t/4)$,
removes $I_1(z)/z$ from the covariance integrands exactly. The
$(t,\beta)$-box layer is Proposition~\ref{prop:localbox}; the global
$[6,15]$ coverage remains the pending content of the relay row.
\end{lemma}

\begin{lemma}[exact; the $S$-free relative criterion]\label{lem:sfree}
Let $S=\sin(t/2)$, $E=\langle N\rangle/\langle D\rangle$, and suppose
$\langle D\rangle>0$. Then for all $t\in(0,\pi)$ and $\beta>0$,
\[
\frac{E'(t,\beta)}{-\tfrac12 S}
=\frac{\bigl\langle \cos 2s+\cos\alpha\cos s\bigr\rangle
+8\beta^3\!\iint (N-E\,D)(1-P-Q)\,H_B(z)\,ds\,d\alpha}
{\langle D\rangle},
\]
and consequently
\[
E'<-\tfrac14 S
\iff
\mathcal C:=\bigl\langle \cos 2s+\cos\alpha\cos s\bigr\rangle
+8\beta^3\!\iint (N-E\,D)(1-P-Q)H_B
-\tfrac12\langle D\rangle>0 .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Since $D_t=0$,
$E'=\bigl[\langle N_t\rangle+\iint (N-E D)\,\partial_tK\bigr]/
\langle D\rangle$. The first term factors $S$ exactly:
$N_t=-\tfrac{S}{2}(\cos 2s+\cos\alpha\cos s)$. So does the second:
$\partial_tK=K\,\partial_t\!\log K=32\beta^3 c\,c'\,(1-P-Q)H_B(z)$ with
$c=\cos(t/4)$, and
$c\,c'=\cos(t/4)\cdot\bigl(-\tfrac14\sin(t/4)\bigr)=-\tfrac{S}{8}$
by the double angle, so $\partial_tK=-4\beta^3S\,(1-P-Q)H_B$. Dividing
by $-\tfrac12 S$ cancels $S$ identically and yields the display. For
the equivalence: the denominator $-\tfrac12 S$ is negative on
$(0,\pi)$, so $E'<-\tfrac14 S$ is the \emph{lower} bound
$E'/(-\tfrac12 S)>\tfrac12$, i.e.\ $\mathcal C>0$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[exact; the bilinear quotient-free form]
\label{cor:chat}
With $\Phi:=\cos 2s+\cos\alpha\cos s$ \textup{(}the letter $A$ stays
reserved for the scaled Bessel factor $e^{-z}I_1(z)/z$\textup{)},
$J_N:=\iint N(1-P-Q)H_B\,ds\,d\alpha$,
$J_D:=\iint D(1-P-Q)H_B\,ds\,d\alpha$, and $\langle D\rangle>0$,
\[
E'<-\tfrac14\sin(t/2)
\iff
\widehat{\mathcal C}:=\langle D\rangle\langle \Phi\rangle
+8\beta^3\bigl(\langle D\rangle J_N-\langle N\rangle J_D\bigr)
-\tfrac12\langle D\rangle^2>0 .
\]
\end{corollary}

\begin{proof}
Multiply $\mathcal C$ of Lemma~\ref{lem:sfree} by $\langle D\rangle>0$
and substitute $E=\langle N\rangle/\langle D\rangle$: the quotient
disappears by bilinearity, and the inequality direction is preserved.
$\widehat{\mathcal C}$ is a bilinear combination of five integrals
with no quotient anywhere; it is the assembly's sole target.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[why $\widehat{\mathcal C}$ is the assembly's object]
\label{rem:sfree}
In $\widehat{\mathcal C}$ the variable $t$ enters only through
$c=\cos(t/4)\in(\tfrac{\sqrt2}{2},1)$: no kinematically forced
vanishing factor and no singularity anywhere in the relay range ---
the common linear zero of margin and correction at $t\to0$ has been
cancelled identically. The endpoint degeneracy is thus removed
structurally; uniform positivity still requires explicit uniform
estimates, which are the assembly's content. The first-order calibration
constant is
$a(t)=\bigl[\tfrac12+\tfrac1{8c^2}\bigr]/c\le\tfrac{3\sqrt2}{4}
\approx1.061$ (supremum at $c=\sqrt2/2$), so the first-order
threshold is $2\sup a\approx2.12$; the target of the analytic relay
is $\widehat{\mathcal C}>0$ uniformly for $\beta\ge\beta_0$ with the
remainder included. At the calibration point $(t,\beta)=(1.5,8)$ the measured
value is $\mathcal C/\langle D\rangle\approx0.417$
(\emph{verified}), against the threshold $0$. The built-in unit
test: the $1/\beta$ expansion of
$\mathcal C/\langle D\rangle+\tfrac12$ must reproduce
$1-a(t)/\beta$; the audited quantity is $\beta\cdot(\text{deviation
from first order})$, which must converge (an $O(\beta^{-2})$
remainder halves the \emph{$\beta$-scaled} deviation when $\beta$
doubles, and divides the unscaled deviation by four).
\end{remark}

\begin{lemma}[exact; far-region pointwise and kernel bounds]
\label{lem:regionIII}
\textup{(a)} $\min_{s,\alpha}\Phi
=-\tfrac98$ exactly: with $u=|\cos s|$ the minimum of
$2u^2-u-1$ on $[0,1]$ is attained at $u=\tfrac14$ and equals
$-\tfrac98$. \textup{(b)} $|D|\le2$ and, by
Corollary~\ref{cor:Ebound} \textup{(}$|E|<1$\textup{)},
$|N-ED|\le|N|+|D|\le(2|C|+1)+2\le5$ and
$|1-P-Q|\le1$. \textup{(c)} On the deficit region
$\{(s,\alpha):2c-R\ge\Delta_0\}$ one has $z=2\beta R\le
z_s-2\beta\Delta_0$ with $z_s=4\beta c$, and since
$A(z)=e^{-z}I_1(z)/z$ is strictly decreasing with
$A(0^+)=\tfrac12$,
\[
K=2\beta\,e^{z}A(z)\;\le\;\beta\,
e^{z_s}\,e^{-2\beta\Delta_0}
\quad\text{on the region.}
\]
The far-region contributions of all five integrals of
Corollary~\ref{cor:chat} are then bounded by the two tail families
of Lemma~\ref{lem:tails} below.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a) $\cos\alpha\cos s\ge-|\cos s|$ with equality attainable, and
$\cos2s=2u^2-1$; minimizing $g(u)=2u^2-u-1$ gives $g(\tfrac14)
=-\tfrac98$. (b) Triangle inequalities; $|N|\le|C|\,|\cos2s|
+|\cos\alpha|\,(|C\cos s|+\sin^2s)\le2|C|+1$. (c) $R\le2c-\Delta_0$
gives $z\le z_s-2\beta\Delta_0$; write $K=I_1(z)/R=2\beta I_1(z)/z
=2\beta e^zA(z)$ and use the monotonicity of $A$ (Section on the two
scaled functions) with $A(z)\le A(0^+)=\tfrac12$ uniformly.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; the two tail families]\label{lem:tails}
$0<H_B(z)\le\tfrac14 I_1(z)/z=\tfrac14e^zA(z)\le\tfrac18e^z$
\textup{(}term by term: the coefficient of $(z/2)^{2k}$ in $H_B$ is
$\tfrac14\frac1{(k+2)\,k!\,(k+1)!}\le\frac1{8\,k!\,(k+1)!}$, the
coefficient in $\tfrac14I_1(z)/z$ being $\frac1{8\,k!\,(k+1)!}$%
\textup{)}. Consequently, writing $\delta X$ for the far-region part
of an integral $X$ over $\{2c-R\ge\Delta_0\}$,
\[
|\delta\langle f\rangle|\le4\pi^2\,|f|_\infty\,\beta\,
e^{z_s-2\beta\Delta_0},
\qquad
|\delta J_f|\le\tfrac{\pi^2}{2}\,|f(1-P-Q)|_\infty\,
e^{z_s-2\beta\Delta_0},
\]
for the three $K$-integrals and the two $H_B$-integrals
respectively; on the same region $z\le z_s-2\beta\Delta_0$ also
bounds $e^z$ in $H_B\le\tfrac18e^z$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proposition}[exact; Region III propagation]\label{prop:regIII}
Let $\widehat{\mathcal C}_{\rm near}$ denote $\widehat{\mathcal C}$
with every integral restricted to $\{2c-R<\Delta_0\}$. Then for every
$\Delta_0\in(0,2c)$ and $\beta\ge1$,
\[
\bigl|\widehat{\mathcal C}-\widehat{\mathcal C}_{\rm near}\bigr|
\;\le\;576\,\pi^4\beta^4\,e^{2z_s}\,e^{-2\beta\Delta_0}.
\]
\end{proposition}

\begin{proof}
Global masses: $\iint K\le4\pi^2\beta e^{z_s}=:M$ and
$\iint H_B\le\tfrac{\pi^2}{2}e^{z_s}=:M_H$ (the pointwise bounds
$K\le\beta e^{z_s}$, $H_B\le\tfrac18e^{z_s}$ hold on all of
$[-\pi,\pi]^2$ since $z\le z_s$). With the pointwise constants of
Lemma~\ref{lem:regionIII} ($|\Phi|_\infty\le2$, $|D|_\infty\le2$,
$|N|_\infty\le3$, $|N(1-P-Q)|_\infty\le3$,
$|D(1-P-Q)|_\infty\le2$), Lemma~\ref{lem:tails} gives
$|\delta\langle D\rangle|,|\delta\langle\Phi\rangle|\le2a$,
$|\delta\langle N\rangle|\le3a$, $|\delta J_N|\le3b$,
$|\delta J_D|\le2b$, where $a:=Me^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$,
$b:=M_He^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$
\textup{(}note $4\pi^2\beta e^{z_s-2\beta\Delta_0}=a$ and
$\tfrac{\pi^2}2e^{z_s-2\beta\Delta_0}=b$\textup{)}. For any product,
$|XY-X_{\rm n}Y_{\rm n}|\le|\delta X|\,|Y|_{\max}
+|X|_{\max}|\delta Y|$ with $|\langle f\rangle|\le|f|_\infty M$,
$|J_f|\le|f(1-P-Q)|_\infty M_H$. Summing:
$\langle D\rangle\langle\Phi\rangle$ contributes $\le8aM$;
$\langle D\rangle J_N$ and $\langle N\rangle J_D$ contribute
$\le12MM_He^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$ each \textup{(}using
$aM_H=bM=MM_He^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$\textup{)};
$\tfrac12\langle D\rangle^2$ contributes $\le4aM$. Total:
$12aM+192\beta^3MM_He^{-2\beta\Delta_0}
=\bigl(192\pi^4\beta^2+384\pi^4\beta^4\bigr)
e^{2z_s}e^{-2\beta\Delta_0}
\le576\pi^4\beta^4e^{2z_s}e^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$ for $\beta\ge1$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[the choice of $\Delta_0$ belongs to the assembly]
With these deliberately crude (but fully named) constants, at
$\beta=15$ the bound forces $\Delta_0\approx1.2$ rather than the
design value $0.3$: the natural saddle-mass scale is
$\langle D\rangle\asymp e^{z_s}\beta^{-3/2}$ (saddle height
$\asymp e^{z_s}\beta^{-1/2}$ times a two-dimensional Gaussian area
$\asymp\beta^{-1}$), so $\langle D\rangle^2\asymp
e^{2z_s}\beta^{-3}$ and the comparison quotient carries
$\beta^{7}e^{-2\beta\Delta_0}$, consuming
$\approx34$ of exponent at $\beta=15$. By Lemma~\ref{lem:mass}
\textup{(}$m_*\ge\tfrac12$\textup{)},
$\Delta_0=1.2$ suffices with margin
(Corollary~\ref{cor:regIIInum}). The enlargement changes no leading
bound, subject to the forthcoming geometric shell estimate: the
shell technique that will bound Region II is insensitive to its
outer edge --- the telescoping sum over deficit shells is
controlled by the kernel mass beyond the \emph{inner} radius
$\Delta_1$ (up to its geometric closing factor, part of that
estimate). Since $\Delta_0<2c$ is free, the constant hunt stays
honest.
\end{remark}

\begin{convention}[cited constants travel with their normalization]

$(2.5,15)$ value computed with the bracket $D/2$ in place of $D$

\end{convention}

\begin{lemma}[exact; analytic lower bound for $I_1$]
\label{lem:I1low}
For $z\ge2$,
$I_1(z)\ge\frac{e^z}{\sqrt{2\pi z}}\bigl(1-\frac1{2z}\bigr)
-\frac32$; consequently
$I_1(z)\ge\frac{e^z}{\sqrt{2\pi z}}\bigl(1-\frac{0.6}{z}\bigr)$
for $z\ge10$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
From $I_1(z)=\frac1\pi\int_0^\pi e^{z\cos\theta}\cos\theta\,
d\theta$ and $u=\sin(\theta/2)$,
$I_1(z)=\frac{2e^z}{\pi}\int_0^1e^{-2zu^2}
\frac{1-2u^2}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\,du$. On $[0,\tfrac1{\sqrt2}]$ drop
$\frac1{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\ge1$ (there $1-2u^2\ge0$) and complete the
Gaussian: $\int_0^\infty e^{-2zu^2}(1-2u^2)\,du=\tfrac12
\sqrt{\tfrac{\pi}{2z}}\bigl(1-\tfrac1{2z}\bigr)$, at the cost of
the Gaussian residue, derived by $u^2=\tfrac12+v$
\textup{(}so $2u^2-1=2v$, $du\le dv/\sqrt2$\textup{)}:
$\int_{1/\sqrt2}^\infty e^{-2zu^2}(2u^2-1)\,du\le
\sqrt2\,e^{-z}\!\int_0^\infty ve^{-2zv}dv=
\frac{e^{-z}}{2\sqrt2\,z^2}\le\tfrac{3}{z}e^{-z}$. On $[\tfrac1{\sqrt2},1]$,
$|1-2u^2|\le1$ and $e^{-2zu^2}\le e^{-z}$ give at most
$\tfrac{\pi}{4}e^{-z}$. Multiplying by $\tfrac{2e^z}{\pi}$: the
main term is $\frac{e^z}{\sqrt{2\pi z}}(1-\tfrac1{2z})$, the two
tails contribute at most $\tfrac12+\tfrac{6}{\pi z}\le\tfrac32$ for
$z\ge2$. For the corollary: at $z\ge10$,
$\frac{e^z}{\sqrt{2\pi z}}\cdot\frac{0.1}{z}\ge
\frac{e^{10}}{\sqrt{20\pi}}\cdot\frac{1}{100}\approx27.8\ge\frac32$,
and the left side increases in $z$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; global upper companion for $I_1$]
\label{lem:I1up}
For every $z>0$, $I_1(z)\le e^z/\sqrt{2\pi z}$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
In the representation of Lemma~\ref{lem:I1low},
$I_1(z)=\frac{2e^z}{\pi}\int_0^1e^{-2zu^2}
\frac{1-2u^2}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\,du$, the integrand is $\le0$ on
$[\tfrac1{\sqrt2},1]$; on $[0,\tfrac1{\sqrt2}]$ one has
$\frac{1-2u^2}{\sqrt{1-u^2}}\le1$: squaring is legitimate there
($1-2u^2\ge0$) and reduces the claim to $u^2(4u^2-3)\le0$, true for
$u^2\le\tfrac34$. Hence
$I_1(z)\le\frac{2e^z}{\pi}\int_0^\infty e^{-2zu^2}\,du
=\frac{2e^z}{\pi}\cdot\frac12\sqrt{\frac{\pi}{2z}}
=\frac{e^z}{\sqrt{2\pi z}}$. \textup{(}The companions of
Lemma~\ref{lem:companions} sharpen this for $z\ge20$; the point of
the global form is the range $z<20$, which the rest bound of
Lemma~\ref{lem:mass} genuinely visits.\textup{)}
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; the area lemma]\label{lem:area}
For $0\le u<1$,
\[
\operatorname{Area}\bigl\{(s,\alpha)\in[-\pi,\pi]^2:\ P+Q\le u\bigr\}
\;\le\;\frac{4\pi u}{\sqrt{1-u}},
\]
and the same bound holds for the mirror set
$\{\tilde P+\tilde Q\le u\}$, $\tilde P:=1-P$, $\tilde Q:=1-Q$.
Consequently, for $c^2\ge\tfrac12$ \textup{(}so
$w\ge g:=P+Q-2PQ$\textup{)},
\[
\operatorname{Area}\{w\le v\}\;\le\;\bar A(v):=
\min\Bigl(\frac{16\pi v}{\sqrt{1-2v}}\Big|_{v<\frac12},\;
4\pi^3v\Bigr),
\]
with $\bar A$ nondecreasing \textup{(}a minimum of two increasing
branches; they cross at $v_c=\tfrac12(1-16/\pi^4)=0.41787\ldots$%
\textup{)}.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
On $[0,\pi]^2$ the substitution $dP=\sqrt{P(1-P)}\,ds$ gives
\[
\operatorname{Area}_{[0,\pi]^2}\{P+Q\le u\}
=\iint_{P+Q\le u}\frac{dP\,dQ}{\sqrt{PQ(1-P)(1-Q)}}
\le\frac{1}{\sqrt{1-u}}\iint_{P+Q\le u}\frac{dP\,dQ}{\sqrt{PQ}}
=\frac{\pi u}{\sqrt{1-u}}
\]
\textup{(}$(1-P)(1-Q)\ge1-u$ on the region; the last integral is
$u$ times the exact Beta integral
$\Gamma(\tfrac12)^2/\Gamma(2)=\pi$\textup{)}; the full square is
four copies, and the mirror set is the image under
$(s,\alpha)\mapsto(\pi-s,\pi-\alpha)$. For the deficit sublevels:
$g$ is invariant under the mirror substitution and
$g\ge\tfrac12(P+Q)$ where $P+Q\le1$, so
$\{g\le v\}\subset\{P+Q\le2v\}\cup\{\tilde P+\tilde Q\le2v\}$ and
the first branch follows; globally $P\ge(s/\pi)^2$ \textup{(}%
concavity of $\sin$\textup{)} puts each component inside a region
of area $2\pi^3v$.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[the sign gap, exposed by the involution]
\label{rem:signgap}
A restricted-domain bound $\int_{B}KD\ge(\ldots)$ on a rectangle
$B$ around the saddle cannot by itself minorize
$\langle D\rangle=\iint KD$, because $D$ changes sign on the
torus: under the exact involution
$T(s,\alpha)=(\pi-s,\pi-\alpha)$ one has $D\circ T=-D$ and
$K_c\circ T=K_{s_4}$ with $s_4=\sin(t/4)$, so the mirror ball
contributes $\int_{T(B)}K_cD=-\int_BK_{s_4}D<0$ --- a negative
part the restricted-domain argument omitted. The owed repair is the
\emph{signed minoration}
$\langle D\rangle\ge L_{\rm main}(c)-U_{\rm mirror}(s_4)
-U_{\rm rest}$, where the mirror term carries the exact relative
suppression $e^{-4\beta(c-s_4)}\le e^{-4C_{\rm win}/\pi}
\approx0.148$ at the moving boundary \textup{(}via
$c-s_4=\sqrt2\sin\tfrac{\pi-t}{4}\ge\tfrac{\pi-t}{\pi}$ from
$\sin y\ge\tfrac{2\sqrt2}{\pi}y$ on $[0,\tfrac\pi4]$\textup{)} ---
order one, not negligible; note also $s_4\in(0,\tfrac{\sqrt2}2)$
falls \emph{outside} the main range, so the mirror-ball upper
bound needs its own (upper) chain --- supplied by step
\textup{(iii)} of the proof of Lemma~\ref{lem:mass}. The measured

\textup{(}slack $2.2$--$4.2\times$: a floor, not an

\end{remark}

\subsection*{The two scaled functions}

The certificate's precision rests on three one-line facts. \emph{(a)}
$A(z)=e^{-z}I_1(z)/z$ is strictly decreasing on $(0,\infty)$: by the
classical lower bound $I_{\nu+1}(z)/I_\nu(z)\ge
z/\bigl(\nu+1+\sqrt{(\nu+1)^2+z^2}\bigr)$, valid for $z>0$ and
$\nu\ge0$ (Amos \cite{Amos}; both members of the classical two-sided
pair are proved in \cite{Segura2011}, Theorem~1), taken at $\nu=0$,
together with $\sqrt{1+z^2}\le z+1$ (square both sides),
$I_1/I_0>z/(z+2)$, which is
exactly $(\log A)'=I_0/I_1-1-2/z<0$; hence $A$ is enclosed over any
$z$-interval by its two endpoint values. \emph{(b)} The kernel--score
product collapses algebraically,
$K\,\partial_t\!\log K=32\beta^3\cos(t/4)(\cos(t/4))'(1-P-Q)\,H_B(z)$
(the factor $I_1(z)/z$ cancels between $K$ and the denominator of the
regular score), so the covariance integrands involve only
$B(z)=e^{-z}H_B(z)$. \emph{(c)} $B$ is enclosed \emph{unconditionally}
by $[e^{-z_{\rm hi}}H_B(z_{\rm lo}),\,e^{-z_{\rm lo}}H_B(z_{\rm hi})]$,
since $e^{-z}$ decreases and $H_B$ (a positive-coefficient entire
series) increases; after centering, the $B$-terms are subdominant and this
crude-but-safe bound suffices. Together with the exact linear-exponential
cell integrals, every source of exponential interval growth is either
integrated analytically or carried by a monotone one-dimensional
profile --- the certificate is structure, not brute force.



\section{The spectral framework}\label{sec:frame}

\begin{convention}[normalizations]\label{conv:norm}
On $(0,\pi)$ define the kernel, entry law, and smoothing operator
\begin{align}
q_1(u,v) &= 2\,e^{\beta\cos u\cos v}\sinh(\beta\sin u\sin v)
          \;=\; 4\sum_{m\ge1}I_m\sin(mu)\sin(mv), \label{eq:q1}\\
s_1(u)   &= \tfrac{\beta}{2}\sin u\,e^{\beta\cos u}
          \;=\; \sum_{m\ge1}m\,I_m\sin(mu), \label{eq:s1}\\
(Qf)(v)  &= \frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^\pi q_1(v,u)f(u)\dd u ,\label{eq:Q}
\end{align}
so that $Q\sin(m\cdot)=I_m\sin(m\cdot)$. Both closed forms are classical
consequences of the generating function
$e^{\beta\cos\theta}=I_0+2\sum_{m\ge1}I_m\cos(m\theta)$; \eqref{eq:s1}
follows by differentiation. All ten verification artifacts in the
repository use exactly these normalizations.
\end{convention}

Since $q_1>0$ on $(0,\pi)^2$ and $s_1>0$ on $(0,\pi)$, the walk with step
kernel $q_1$ killed on $\{0,\pi\}$ is well defined; $H(\psi)=\beta
\sin(\psi/2)I_1(2\beta\cos(\psi/2))$ and $K_2(\varphi)=I_0(2\beta
\cos(\varphi/2))$ appear below. Note $\FB=Q^3s_1$ and, by the sine
orthogonality computation of Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge}, $\FA$ is the
companion cosine-weighted integral.

\section{Theorem A: positivity of $\FB$}\label{sec:thmA}

\begin{theorem}[exact]\label{thm:A}
$\FB(t)>0$ for all $t\in(0,\pi)$ and all $\beta>0$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}[First proof (kernel positivity)]
$\FB=Q^3s_1$ by \eqref{eq:Q} and $b_m=mI_m^4$. Each application of $Q$
integrates a positive function against the strictly positive kernel
\eqref{eq:q1}; $s_1>0$ on $(0,\pi)$. Hence $\FB>0$ pointwise.
\end{proof}

\begin{proof}[Second proof (symmetric-decreasing convolution)]
With $K_2(\varphi)=I_0(2\beta\cos(\varphi/2))$ and $P_4=K_2*K_2$ (circle
convolution; Section~\ref{sec:reduction}), $\FB=-\tfrac12P_4'$. The
function $K_2$ is even and \emph{strictly decreasing in $|\varphi|$ on
$[0,\pi]$} ($I_0$ strictly increasing, $\cos(\varphi/2)$ strictly
decreasing). By the layer-cake representation, $K_2=\int_0^\infty
\mathbf 1_{A_\lambda}\dd\lambda$ with $A_\lambda$ centered arcs whose
half-lengths $a(\lambda)$ fill $(0,\pi)$ continuously (strict decrease).
For centered arcs, $(\mathbf 1_{A}*\mathbf 1_{B})(t)=|A\cap(t+B)|$ is
even and nonincreasing in $|t|$ on $[0,\pi]$, and \emph{strictly}
decreasing on $||a-b|,\min(a+b,2\pi-a-b)|$. Hence
$P_4(t)=\iint |A_\lambda\cap(t+A_\mu)|\dd\lambda\dd\mu$ is even,
nonincreasing on $[0,\pi]$, and strictly decreasing on $(0,\pi)$ because
for every $t\in(0,\pi)$ a positive measure of pairs $(\lambda,\mu)$ has
$|a(\lambda)-a(\mu)|<t<\min(a+b,2\pi-a-b)$ (the half-lengths fill
$(0,\pi)$). At the derivative level: each overlap $t\mapsto|A_\lambda\cap(t+A_\mu)|$
is Lipschitz with a.e.\ derivative $-1$ in the strict regime and $0$
otherwise (on $[0,\pi]$); since $P_4$ is analytic, dominated convergence
gives $P_4'(t)=\iint\partial_t|A_\lambda\cap(t+A_\mu)|\dd\lambda\dd\mu
\le-\,\mu\{(\lambda,\mu):\text{strict at }t\}<0$ pointwise on $(0,\pi)$.
Thus $\FB=-\tfrac12P_4'>0$ on $(0,\pi)$.
\end{proof}

\section{Theorem B: ratio monotonicity for $0<\beta\le3$}\label{sec:thmB}

\begin{lemma}[exact; Wronskian identity]\label{lem:wronskian}
For real arrays with $\sum m(|a_m|+|b_m|)<\infty$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:wronskian}
W:=2(\FA'\FB-\FA\FB')=\sum_{1\le m<n}c_{mn}\,T_{mn}(t),\qquad
c_{mn}=a_mb_n-a_nb_m,
\end{equation}
where $T_{mn}(t)=(m-n)\sin((m+n)t)+(m+n)\sin((n-m)t)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Product-to-sum: $T_{mn}=2[m\cos(mt)\sin(nt)-n\sin(mt)\cos(nt)]=:2G(m,n)$,
antisymmetric with $G(n,n)=0$; since $c_{mn}$ is antisymmetric,
$\sum_{m<n}c_{mn}2G=2\sum_{m,n}a_mb_nG(m,n)=2[\FA'\FB-\FA\FB']$
\textup{(}first recorded in \cite{WronskianNote}\textup{)}.
\end{proof}

The coefficient input is the weighted Tur\'an-type lemma
(Appendix~\ref{app:phi}, self-contained): $\varphi_m=a_m/b_m$ is strictly
increasing in $m\ge1$ for every $\beta>0$, hence \emph{every minor is
negative}: $c_{mn}<0$ for $m<n$ (exact).

\begin{lemma}[exact; the pair identities and bounds]\label{lem:pairs}
For $t\in(0,\pi)$, with $p=n-m$, $q=n+m$:
\begin{itemize}
\item[(a)] $T_{12}=4\sin^3t$;\quad $T_{13}=16\sin^3t\,\cos t$;\quad
$T_{24}=8\sin^3(2t)$, so $|T_{13}|\le16\sin^3t$ and
$|T_{24}|\le64\sin^3t$.
\item[(b)] $T_{m,m+1}\ge0$ for every $m\ge1$.
\item[(c)] $|T_{mn}|\le\dfrac{\pi^3}{48}\,pq(q^2+p^2)\,\sin^3t$ for all
$m<n$.
\end{itemize}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(a) $3\sin u-\sin3u=4\sin^3u$ gives $T_{12}$ (at $u=t$) and $T_{24}$ (at
$u=2t$); $T_{13}=4\sin2t-2\sin4t=4\sin2t(1-\cos2t)=16\sin^3t\cos t$.
(b) $T_{m,m+1}=(2m+1)\sin t-\sin((2m+1)t)\ge0$ since
$|\sin(qt)|\le q\sin t$ on $(0,\pi)$ (induction on $q$).
(c) $T_{mn}$ and its first two derivatives vanish at $t=0$ and $t=\pi$
(the parities of $m\pm n$ agree), and $|T_{mn}'''|\le
pq^3+qp^3=pq(q^2+p^2)$; hence
$|T_{mn}(t)|\le\frac{pq(q^2+p^2)}{6}\min(t,\pi-t)^3$, and
$\sin t\ge\frac2\pi\min(t,\pi-t)$ gives the claim.
\end{proof}

\begin{theorem}[exact modulo the certified computation
\texttt{certify\_thmB.py}]\label{thm:B}
For $0<\beta\le3$:\; $W<0$ on $(0,\pi)$, hence $E'<0$ on $(0,\pi)$.
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Drop the adjacent pairs $m\ge2$ (each contributes $c_{m,m+1}T_{m,m+1}
\le0$ by Lemma~\ref{lem:pairs}(b) and $c<0$); keep $T_{12}$, $T_{13}$,
$T_{24}$ exactly and majorize every other pair by
Lemma~\ref{lem:pairs}(c). Pointwise in $t$,
\[
\frac{W(t)}{\sin^3t}\;\le\;\mathrm{Crit}(\beta):=
-4|c_{12}|+16|c_{13}|+64|c_{24}|
+\frac{\pi^3}{48}\!\!\sum_{\substack{p\ge2\\(m,n)\ne(1,3),(2,4)}}\!\!
pq(q^2+p^2)\,|c_{mn}| .
\]
The certificate \texttt{certify\_thmB.py} (Section~\ref{sec:verif})
encloses $\mathrm{Crit}$ in interval arithmetic over adaptive
$\beta$-boxes covering $[1/20,3]$ ($I_m$ increasing in $\beta$ gives
rigorous boxes; cutoff $M=60$ with the tail itself computed in
intervals and closed by a derived, per-box-asserted geometric ratio) and
certifies $\mathrm{Crit}<0$ on all of $[1/20,3]$; the
script then re-certifies \emph{every} box at precision $+70$ before
reporting success (the box count is invocation- and implementation-dependent; the
independent Arb implementation \texttt{certify\_thmB\_arb.py} certifies
the same coverage with $86$ boxes and a full stability pass, transcript
archived). For $0<\beta\le1/20$, Lemma~\ref{lem:smallbeta} applies.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; small $\beta$]\label{lem:smallbeta}
For $0<\beta\le1/20$:\; $\mathrm{Crit}(\beta)<0$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Write $\kappa=\beta/2\le1/40$, $L_m=\kappa^m/m!$,
$U_m=e^*L_m$ with $e^*=e^{\beta^2/4}\le e^{1/1600}<1.001$; then
$L_m\le I_m\le U_m$ (positive series). \emph{Leading minor:}
$a_2b_1\ge(I_1^2I_2^2)(I_1^4)\ge L_1^6L_2^2=\kappa^{10}/4$ and
$a_1b_2=(2I_1^2I_2^2)(2I_2^4)\le4U_1^2U_2^6=(e^*)^8\kappa^{14}/16$,
so
\[
|c_{12}|\ \ge\ \frac{\kappa^{10}}{4}
\Bigl(1-\frac{(e^*)^8\kappa^4}{4}\Bigr)
\ \ge\ \frac{\kappa^{10}}{4}\,(1-10^{-6}).
\]
\emph{The three visible positive terms:}
$a_3\le I_3^2\cdot(2I_2^2+4I_4^2)\le(e^*)^4\kappa^{10}/24\cdot(1+10^{-4})$
and $b_1\le(e^*)^4\kappa^4$ give
$16|c_{13}|\le16(a_3b_1+a_1b_3)\le0.70\,\kappa^{14}$;
$64|c_{24}|\le64(a_4b_2+a_2b_4)\le10^{-3}\kappa^{14}$ (both products
have $\kappa$-degree $\ge22$); the largest remaining weighted pair is
$(1,4)$: $\frac{\pi^3}{48}\cdot510\cdot(a_4b_1+a_1b_4)
\le0.2\,\kappa^{18}\le10^{-6}\kappa^{14}$.
\emph{All other pairs} (those with $s:=m+n\ge6$, $p\ge2$): using
$a_nb_m+a_mb_n\le2mn(e^*)^8\kappa^{4s-2}
[((m-1)!m!)^{-2}n!^{-4}+((n-1)!n!)^{-2}m!^{-4}]
\le s^2(e^*)^8\kappa^{4s-2}$ (the factorial bracket is $\le2$ and
$2mn\le s^2/2$), $pq(q^2+p^2)\le 2s^4$, and at most $s$ pairs per level,
\[
\frac{\pi^3}{48}\!\!\sum_{s\ge6}\!\!\;(\text{level }s)
\le\frac{\pi^3}{48}(e^*)^8\sum_{s\ge6}2s^7\kappa^{4s-2}
\le0.66\,\kappa^{22}\cdot6^7\sum_{j\ge0}\bigl[(7/6)^7\kappa^4\bigr]^j
\le3.7\times10^5\,\kappa^{22}\le10^{-7}\kappa^{14},
\]
where the level ratio $s\mapsto s+1$ is at most
$(7/6)^7\kappa^4<10^{-5}<\tfrac12$ (so the geometric closure is valid),
and $\kappa^{8}\le40^{-8}$ absorbs the constant. Collecting:
\[
\mathrm{Crit}\le-\kappa^{10}(1-10^{-6})+0.71\,\kappa^{14}
=-\kappa^{10}\bigl(1-10^{-6}-0.71\,\kappa^4\bigr)<0,
\]
since $\kappa^4\le40^{-4}<4\cdot10^{-7}$. (All numbered constants above
are crude upper counts; each step loses factors, none gains.)
\end{proof}

\end{remark}

\section{The bridge structure of $E$ (exact)}\label{sec:bridge}

Let $k=q_1\!\circ\!q_1$ denote the two-step kernel,
$k(\psi,t)=4\sum_{m\ge1}I_m^2\sin(m\psi)\sin(mt)$, and define
\begin{equation}\label{eq:omega}
\omega_t(\dd\psi)\;\propto\;(Qs_1)(\psi)\,k(\psi,t)\dd\psi
\qquad\text{on }(0,\pi),
\end{equation}
the law of the time-$2$ position (midpoint) of the four-step bridge
entering from $0$ with law $s_1$ and conditioned to end at $t$.

\begin{lemma}[exact]\label{lem:bridge}
$\displaystyle \int_0^\pi (Qs_1)\,k(\cdot,t)\dd\psi=2\pi\FB(t)$ and
$\displaystyle \int_0^\pi \cos\psi\,(Qs_1)\,k(\cdot,t)\dd\psi=\pi\FA(t)$.
Hence, as an identity,
\[
E(t)\;=\;\mathbb{E}_{\omega_t}[\cos\psi]\;=\;\frac{\FA(t)}{2\FB(t)} .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$(Qs_1)(\psi)=\sum m I_m^2\sin(m\psi)$. By sine orthogonality the first
integral picks the diagonal, giving $2\pi\sum mI_m^4\sin(mt)$. In the
second, $\cos\psi\,\sin(m\psi)\sin(n\psi)$ integrates to
$\frac{\pi}{4}(\delta_{n,m+1}+\delta_{n,m-1})$, which produces exactly
the weights $(n-1)I_{n-1}^2+(n+1)I_{n+1}^2$ of $a_n$.
\end{proof}

\begin{corollary}[exact; $|E|<1$]\label{cor:Ebound}
For all $t\in(0,\pi)$ and $\beta>0$, $|E(t,\beta)|<1$. Indeed
$q_1(u,v)=2e^{\beta\cos u\cos v}\sinh(\beta\sin u\sin v)>0$ on
$(0,\pi)^2$ and $s_1>0$, so $(Qs_1)>0$ and $k=q_1\!\circ\!q_1>0$ on
$(0,\pi)$; hence $\omega_t$ in \eqref{eq:omega} is a genuine
probability measure and
$E=\mathbb{E}_{\omega_t}[\cos\psi]\in(-1,1)$.
\end{corollary}

\begin{lemma}[exact; entry law]\label{lem:entry}
$\partial_t k(0^+,\psi)=2H(\psi)$, where
$H(\psi)=\beta\sin(\psi/2)\,I_1(2\beta\cos(\psi/2))$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Differentiate in $\psi$ the squared Graf identity
$I_0^2+2\sum_{m\ge1}I_m^2\cos(m\psi)=I_0(2\beta\cos(\psi/2))$
\cite[\S10.23(ii)]{DLMF} and compare with
$\partial_tk(0^+,\psi)=4\sum mI_m^2\sin(m\psi)$.
\end{proof}

 -2.5299150980081786690028074&]\times10^{-10}.

Consequently no monotone coupling of full bridge paths exists at

\begin{lemma}[exact; the $3/2$ mechanism]\label{lem:mech}
$\displaystyle

{\beta\sin t\;u}=1+\frac{2\beta}{3}\cos t .$
For $t>\pi/2$ the corner sign change of $\partial_u\partial_t\log q_1$

\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
$\partial_t\log q_1=-\beta\sin t\cos u+\beta\cos t\sin u\coth(\beta\sin

\end{proof}

\section{The single-Bessel reduction and the master formula}
\label{sec:reduction}

\begin{lemma}[exact; Neumann product formulas]\label{lem:neumann}
For $x,y\ge0$, with $w=\sqrt{x^2+y^2+2xy\cos\alpha}$,
\[
I_0(x)I_0(y)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} I_0(w)\dd\alpha,
\qquad
I_0(x)I_1(y)=\frac{1}{2\pi}\int_0^{2\pi} I_1(w)\,\frac{y+x\cos\alpha}{w}
\dd\alpha .
\]
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The first is the integrated Graf/Neumann addition theorem
\cite[\S10.23(ii)]{DLMF}; the second is its $\partial_y$-derivative.
\end{proof}

\begin{remark}[signs]\label{rem:signs}
Both formulas extend to all real $x,y$, as used below (on
$[-\pi,\pi]^2$ one has $c_1=\cos(\varphi/2)\ge0$ but $c_2$ may be
negative): $w(x,-y,\alpha)=w(x,y,\pi-\alpha)$, and the substitution
$\alpha\mapsto\pi-\alpha$ over a full period maps the first identity to
itself (both sides even in $y$) and the second to its negative on both
sides simultaneously ($I_1$ odd; $(-y+x\cos(\pi-\alpha))=
-(y+x\cos\alpha)$).
\end{remark}

With $K_2(\varphi)=I_0(2\beta\cos(\varphi/2))=\sum_{m\in\mathbb Z}I_m^2
e^{im\varphi}$ and $P_4=K_2*K_2$ (circle convolution),
$P_4=\sum_{m}I_m^4e^{imt}$ and $\FB=-\tfrac12P_4'$. Applying
Lemma~\ref{lem:neumann} to both convolution factors:

\begin{lemma}[exact; master representation]\label{lem:master}
Let $s=\varphi-t/2$, $c_1=\cos(\varphi/2)$, $c_2=\cos((t-\varphi)/2)$,
$P=\sin^2(s/2)$, $Q=\sin^2(\alpha/2)$, and
\begin{equation}\label{eq:R2}
R^2=c_1^2+c_2^2+2c_1c_2\cos\alpha
   =4\bigl[\cos^2(t/4)\,(1-P-Q)+PQ\bigr] .
\end{equation}
Then
\begin{equation}\label{eq:FBfull}
\FB(t)=\frac{\beta\sin(t/2)}{8\pi^2}
\iint_{[-\pi,\pi]^2}\frac{I_1(2\beta R)}{R}
\bigl[\cos^2(s/2)-\sin^2(\alpha/2)\bigr]\dd s\dd\alpha,
\end{equation}
and, under the common positive kernel $K=I_1(2\beta R)/R$,
\begin{equation}\label{eq:masterE}
E(t)=\frac{\bigl\langle\,
\cos(t/2)\cos2s+\cos\alpha\,[\cos(t/2)\cos s-\sin^2 s]\,\bigr\rangle}
{\bigl\langle\,\cos s+\cos\alpha\,\bigr\rangle},
\end{equation}
where $\langle\cdot\rangle$ denotes the $K\dd s\dd\alpha$-integral over
$[-\pi,\pi]^2$. The prefactor collapse behind \eqref{eq:FBfull} is the
sum-to-product symmetrization
$\tfrac12[\sin(\tfrac{t-\varphi}{2})(c_2+c_1\cos\alpha)+(\varphi
\leftrightarrow t-\varphi)]=\tfrac12\sin(t/2)(\cos s+\cos\alpha)$, and
$\cos s+\cos\alpha=2[\cos^2(s/2)-\sin^2(\alpha/2)]$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
(i) The squared Graf identity gives $K_2(\varphi)=\sum_mI_m^2
e^{im\varphi}$; convolution multiplies Fourier coefficients, so
$P_4=\sum_mI_m^4e^{im\varphi}$ and, termwise (super-exponential decay),
$\FB=-\tfrac12P_4'$. (ii) Insert Lemma~\ref{lem:neumann} (first formula)
with $x=2\beta c_1$, $y=2\beta c_2$ into $P_4(t)=\frac1{2\pi}\int
K_2(\varphi)K_2(t-\varphi)\dd\varphi$: a two-dimensional integral of
$I_0(2\beta R)$ with $R$ as in \eqref{eq:R2}; the $(P,Q)$ form of $R^2$
is product-to-sum. (iii) Differentiate under the integral (smooth
integrand, compact domain): $\partial_tI_0(2\beta R)=I_1(2\beta R)\cdot
2\beta\,\partial_tR$ and $2R\,\partial_tR=2(c_2+c_1\cos\alpha)\,
\partial_tc_2$ with $\partial_tc_2=-\tfrac12\sin((t-\varphi)/2)$.
(iv) Symmetrize over $\varphi\leftrightarrow t-\varphi$ ($R$ invariant):
with $\sin(x/2)\cos(x/2)=\tfrac12\sin x$ and the angle-addition formula,
\begin{align*}
\tfrac12\bigl[\sin(\tfrac{t-\varphi}{2})(c_2+c_1\cos\alpha)
+\sin(\tfrac{\varphi}{2})(c_1+c_2\cos\alpha)\bigr]
&=\tfrac14[\sin(t-\varphi)+\sin\varphi]
+\tfrac12\sin(\tfrac t2)\cos\alpha\\
&=\tfrac12\sin(\tfrac t2)(\cos s+\cos\alpha),
\end{align*}
using $\sin(t-\varphi)+\sin\varphi=2\sin(t/2)\cos s$ and
$\sin(\tfrac{t-\varphi}{2})c_1+\sin(\tfrac{\varphi}{2})c_2
=\sin(t/2)$. Collecting constants yields \eqref{eq:FBfull}. The same
substitution in the companion representation of $\FA$
(Lemma~\ref{lem:bridge} with Lemma~\ref{lem:neumann}, second formula)
and cancellation of the common factor $\beta\sin(t/2)$ yields
\eqref{eq:masterE}. As an end-to-end check, \eqref{eq:FBfull} and
\eqref{eq:masterE} match the quartic series with ratio
$1.000000000000$ at $t\in\{0.4,1.1,2.3,2.9\}$, $\beta=4$.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; geometry of the $(P,Q)$ square, $0<t<\pi$]
\label{lem:geometry}
On $[0,1]^2$: the main saddle is $(0,0)$ with $R=2\cos(t/4)$; the
secondary saddle is $(1,1)$ with $R=2\sin(t/4)$ and gap
$2(\cos(t/4)-\sin(t/4))>0$ degenerating only at $t=\pi$; the zero set
$\{R=0\}$ consists of exactly the two corners $(1,0)$ and $(0,1)$, and at
both of them \emph{both} brackets of \eqref{eq:FBfull} and
\eqref{eq:masterE} vanish, so the singular points of $K$ are switched off
(the extension $I_1(z)/z\to\tfrac12$ makes $K$ continuous with value
$\beta$). At $t=0$ the zero set degenerates to the edges $P=1$, $Q=1$.
The saddle deficit is the exact sum of two squares
\begin{equation}\label{eq:deficit}
4\cos^2(t/4)-R^2=4\cos^2(t/4)\sin^2(s/2)+4c_1c_2\sin^2(\alpha/2),
\qquad c_1c_2=\cos^2(t/4)-\sin^2(s/2),
\end{equation}
with sign region $c_1c_2\ge0\iff|s|\le\pi-t/2$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
\eqref{eq:R2} and \eqref{eq:deficit} are product-to-sum computations
(symbolic zeros). $R^2\ge(|c_1|-|c_2|)^2$ with equality forcing
$|c_1|=|c_2|$ (i.e.\ $s=0$ or $s=\pm\pi$) and $\cos\alpha=\mp
\mathrm{sign}(c_1c_2)$, which lands exactly on $(P,Q)\in\{(0,1),(1,0)\}$.
Bracket vanishing at both points is direct substitution. The Jacobian of
$(s,\alpha)\mapsto(P,Q)$ carries the arcsine weight
$\dd s\dd\alpha=\dd P\dd Q/\sqrt{P(1-P)Q(1-Q)}$ (per quadrant), singular
at all four corners; near the main saddle one therefore works in
$(s,\alpha)$, and in $(P,Q)$ only for the global geometry.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; saddle cancellations]
\label{lem:cancel}
Write $N,D$ for numerator and denominator brackets of \eqref{eq:masterE},
$C=\cos(t/2)$, $c_0=\cos(t/4)$. At the saddle $(s,\alpha)=(0,0)$:
\[
\frac{N}{D}\Big|_{(0,0)}=C,\qquad
N_{\alpha\alpha}-C\,D_{\alpha\alpha}=0,\qquad
N_{ss}-C\,D_{ss}=-4C-2,
\]
and for $N_t=-\tfrac12\sin(t/2)[\cos2s+\cos\alpha\cos s]$:
\[
N_{t,\alpha\alpha}-m\,D_{\alpha\alpha}=0\ \ (m=-\tfrac12\sin(t/2)),
\qquad N_{t,ss}-m\,D_{ss}=2\sin(t/2).
\]
Also exact: $2C+1=4c_0^2-1$ and $C\,c(t)=2c_0-1/(2c_0)$ for
$c(t)=(4c_0^2-1)/(2c_0C)$.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proposition}[verified; the second-order laws]\label{prop:secondorder}
The frozen-kernel expansion built on Lemma~\ref{lem:cancel} (with
$\langle s^2\rangle=1/(\beta c_0)+O(\beta^{-2})$ from
\eqref{eq:deficit}) predicts
\begin{equation}\label{eq:secondorder}
E=C\Bigl(1-\frac{c(t)}{\beta}\Bigr)+O(\beta^{-2}),\qquad
E'=-\tfrac12\sin(t/2)+\frac{\sin(t/4)(4c_0^2+1)}{8\beta c_0^2}
+O(\beta^{-2}).
\end{equation}
The coefficient identities are exact (Lemma~\ref{lem:cancel}); the
\emph{laws with remainder} are verified, not yet certified: measured
remainders are $\approx0.15/\beta^2$ with true convergence at fixed $t$
(e.g.\ $\beta^2$-scaled residues $0.1776/0.1767/0.1762$ at $t=1$,
$\beta=30/60/120$), and $\beta\,(E'+\tfrac12\sin(t/2))\to
\sin(t/4)(4c_0^2+1)/(8c_0^2)$ at $t=1,2$. The remainder control is
exactly what the closure map owes. Note the $1/\beta$ correction to $E'$
is \emph{positive}: it opposes the target inequality and must be beaten,
not used. The $\alpha$-cancellations imply every kernel correction
multiplies a vanishing fluctuation and enters at $O(\beta^{-2})$ only.
\end{proposition}

\begin{remark}[exact decomposition of $E'$]\label{rem:Eprime}
With the normalized positive measure $\dd\mu\propto K\dd s\dd\alpha$,
$E'=\langle N_t\rangle/\langle D\rangle+\langle(N-ED)\,
\partial_t\log K\rangle/\langle D\rangle$, $D_t=0$, and the regular form
$\partial_t\log K=4cc'(1-P-Q)\,[zI_0(z)/I_1(z)-2]/R^2$, $z=2\beta R$,
extends continuously by $4\beta^2cc'(1-P-Q)$ at $R=0$. Verified split at
$(\beta,t)=(5,1.3)$: $-0.238212-0.024552=-0.262764=$ the finite
difference exactly. Bounds for $E'$ are taken from this exact quotient
--- never by differentiating an estimate of $E$.
\end{remark}

\section{The $\pi$-endpoint package}\label{sec:endpoint}

\begin{lemma}[exact; telescoping]\label{lem:telescope}
With $a_m$ as in \eqref{eq:coeffs}:
\[
A_\infty:=\sum_{m\ge1}(-1)^{m+1}m\,a_m=0,
\qquad
\sum_{m\ge1}(-1)^{m+1}m^3a_m
=-\sum_{m\ge1}(-1)^{m+1}m(m+1)(2m+1)\,I_m^2I_{m+1}^2 .
\]
Hence $\FA(\pi-\varepsilon)=c_3\,\varepsilon^3+O(\varepsilon^5)$ with
\begin{equation}\label{eq:c3}
c_3=\frac16\sum_{m\ge1}(-1)^{m+1}m(m+1)(2m+1)\,I_m^2I_{m+1}^2 .
\end{equation}
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Split $m^ra_m=m^r(m-1)I_{m-1}^2I_m^2+m^r(m+1)I_m^2I_{m+1}^2$ and shift
$m\to m+1$ in the first sum: for $r=1$ the two weights cancel
($m(m+1)-m(m+1)=0$); for $r=3$ they combine to
$m(m+1)[m^2-(m+1)^2]=-m(m+1)(2m+1)$. Absolute convergence justifies the
reindexing; $\sin(m(\pi-\varepsilon))=(-1)^{m+1}\sin(m\varepsilon)$
expands the sine.
\end{proof}

\begin{lemma}[exact; integral form and parity]\label{lem:c3int}
Let $g(u)=\tfrac12 I_1(2\beta\cos u)=\sum_{m\ge0}I_mI_{m+1}
\cos((2m+1)u)$ \textup{(addition theorem)}. Then
\[
c_3=\frac{S_3-S_1}{24},\qquad
S_1=\frac{2}{\pi}\int_0^{\pi}g(\tfrac\pi2-u)\,g'(u)\dd u,\quad
S_3=\frac{2}{\pi}\int_0^{\pi}g'(\tfrac\pi2-u)\,\bigl(-g''(u)\bigr)\dd u .
\]
Moreover $g(-u)=g(u)$ and $g(\pi-u)=-g(u)$ (parity of $I_1$), hence
$g'(\pi-u)=g'(u)$, $g''(\pi-u)=-g''(u)$, and \emph{both} integrands are
invariant under $u\mapsto\pi-u$: the two halves $[0,\pi/2]$ and
$[\pi/2,\pi]$ contribute exactly equally, and one may integrate a half
range and double. In particular no cancellation between the two phase
maxima $u=\pi/4$ and $u=3\pi/4$ is possible --- exactly, not
asymptotically.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
The expansion of $g$ is the Graf addition theorem with unit shift.
Quarter turn: $\cos((2m+1)(\tfrac\pi2-u))=(-1)^m\sin((2m+1)u)$, so
$g(\tfrac\pi2-u)=\sum_m(-1)^mI_mI_{m+1}\sin((2m+1)u)$ while
$g'(u)=-\sum_k(2k+1)I_kI_{k+1}\sin((2k+1)u)$ and
$g''(u)=-\sum_k(2k+1)^2I_kI_{k+1}\cos((2k+1)u)$, whence
$g'(\tfrac\pi2-u)=-\sum_k(-1)^k(2k+1)I_kI_{k+1}\cos((2k+1)u)$.
Orthogonality of $\{\sin((2m+1)u)\}$ and $\{\cos((2m+1)u)\}$ on
$[0,\pi]$ (norm $\pi/2$) gives
$S_1=-\sum_m(-1)^m(2m+1)(I_mI_{m+1})^2$ and
$S_3=-\sum_m(-1)^m(2m+1)^3(I_mI_{m+1})^2$, so
\begin{align*}
\frac{S_3-S_1}{24}
&=\sum_{m\ge0}(-1)^{m+1}\frac{(2m+1)^3-(2m+1)}{24}(I_mI_{m+1})^2\\
&=\frac16\sum_{m\ge1}(-1)^{m+1}m(m+1)(2m+1)I_m^2I_{m+1}^2=c_3,
\end{align*}
using $(2m+1)^3-(2m+1)=4m(m+1)(2m+1)$ and the vanishing of the $m=0$
term. The parity claims follow from $I_1(-z)=-I_1(z)$ applied to
$g(\pi-u)=\tfrac12I_1(-2\beta\cos u)$, and by differentiating the two
symmetry relations.
\end{proof}

\begin{proposition}[verified; prefactor law]\label{prop:prefactor}
$c_3>0$ for $\beta\in\{1,5,20,40,80,120\}$ at cancellation-adjusted
precision \textup{(the alternating sum cancels at scale
$e^{-(4-2\sqrt2)\beta}$; evaluations use $\mathrm{dps}\ge0.6\beta+50$)},
and
\[
c_3\;\sim\;A\,\beta^{3/2}e^{2\sqrt2\beta},\qquad
A=\frac{1}{24\cdot2^{1/4}\pi^{3/2}}=0.0062922\ldots,
\]
with measured ratios $0.0058646,\,0.0060049,\,0.0060996,\,0.0061475$ at
$\beta=40,60,90,120$ and $O(1/\beta)$ gaps. The certified large-$\beta$
branch (pending) must produce the signed prefactor with an explicit
$B$: $I_{\rm half}\ge\frac{A}{2}\beta^{3/2}e^{2\sqrt2\beta}(1-B/\beta)
-T(\beta)$, $c_3=2I_{\rm half}$, read $\beta^*$, and close
$[15,B_{\rm fin}]$, $B_{\rm fin}=\max\{\beta^*,10C\}$, by intervals.
\end{proposition}

\section{The weighted Tur\'an-type lemma, self-contained}\label{app:phi}

\begin{theorem}[exact]\label{thm:phi}
For every $x>0$ and every real $m\ge1$, with $\varphi_m=a_m/b_m$ built
from \eqref{eq:coeffs} at argument $x$:\; $\varphi_m<\varphi_{m+1}$.
Equivalently, with $\rho_\mu=I_{\mu+1}/I_\mu$,
\[
\frac1m\Bigl(\frac{m-1}{\rho_{m-1}^2}+(m+1)\rho_m^2\Bigr)
<\frac1{m+1}\Bigl(\frac{m}{\rho_m^2}+(m+2)\rho_{m+1}^2\Bigr).
\]
\end{theorem}

\begin{proof}
Two classical inputs, valid for all real orders: the three-term
recurrence $\rho_{m-1}^{-1}=\rho_m+2m/x$, $\rho_{m+1}=\rho_m^{-1}
-2(m+1)/x$ \cite[10.29.1]{DLMF}, and the Amos-type bound
\[
\rho_m(x)<U_m(x):=\frac{x}{a+\sqrt{a^2+x^2}},\qquad a=m+\tfrac12,
\]
proved best of its form for all real orders $\ge0$ by Segura
\cite{Segura2011}; we use \cite[Thm.~2]{RuizSegura}, which bounds
$I_\nu(x)/I_{\nu-1}(x)$ for $\nu\ge\tfrac12$ by
$x/\bigl(\nu-\tfrac12+\sqrt{(\nu+\tfrac12)^2+x^2}\,\bigr)$; setting
$\nu=m+1$ bounds $\rho_m=I_{m+1}/I_m$ by
$x/\bigl(m+\tfrac12+\sqrt{(m+\tfrac32)^2+x^2}\,\bigr)
\le x/\bigl(a+\sqrt{a^2+x^2}\,\bigr)$ with $a=m+\tfrac12$, which is the
displayed bound (the last step uses $\sqrt{(m+\tfrac32)^2+x^2}\ge
\sqrt{a^2+x^2}$).
Substitute the recurrences, write $u=\rho_m\in(0,1)$, $c=1/x$,
$S=u+1/u$, $P=1/u-u$; clearing the positive denominators, the claim
becomes $\Delta_m>0$ where the \emph{exact factorization}
\[
\frac{\Delta_m}{2m(m+1)}=(S-3c)\bigl(P-(2m+1)c\bigr)+(2m+1)c^2
\]
holds as an algebraic identity (expand and collect; the coefficients of
$u^{\pm2}$ are $\pm2m(m+1)$, the linear-in-$c$ terms are
$-4m(m+1)c[(m-1)u+(m+2)/u]$, and $(m-1)u+(m+2)/u=\tfrac12[(2m+1)S+3P]$,
$1/u^2-u^2=PS$). The second factor is positive because $U_m$ is the
positive root of $1/w-w=(2m+1)/x$ (the calibration $1/U_m-U_m=2a/x$) and
$w\mapsto1/w-w$ is strictly decreasing, so $\rho_m<U_m$ gives
$P>(2m+1)c$. The first factor is positive because $\sqrt{a^2+x^2}>a$
gives $U_m<x/(2a)$, so $u<x/(2m+1)\le x/3$ and
$S>1/u>(2m+1)/x\ge3c$. The third term is positive. Strictness is strict
throughout. \textup{(}This is the proof of \cite{PhiLemma}, reproduced
so that Theorem~\ref{thm:B} is self-contained; the ordered-field
implication is machine-checked in \texttt{PhiMonotone.lean}, modulo the
two classical inputs carried as named hypotheses.\textup{)}
\end{proof}
